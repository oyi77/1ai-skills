# Personal Productivity

Framework for managing time, energy, and focus: GTD, Eisenhower Matrix, time blocking, deep work, and energy management.

## When to Use

- Feeling overwhelmed by tasks
- Struggling with focus or procrastination
- Optimizing daily/weekly routines
- Balancing multiple priorities
- **When NOT to use**: Team productivity issues (use project management), or strategic planning (use OKRs)

## GTD (Getting Things Done)

**Principle**: Get tasks out of your head into a trusted system.

**5 Steps**:

### 1. Capture

**Goal**: Collect everything that has your attention.

**How**: Inbox (email, notes app, paper) for ALL inputs.

**Example**: Brain dump everything (work tasks, personal errands, ideas).

---

### 2. Clarify

**Goal**: Process each item (what is it? what's next?).

**Decision tree**:
- **Actionable?**
  - **Yes**: Can you do it in <2 min? → Do it now. Else → Defer to list.
  - **No**: Is it reference? → File it. Trash it? → Delete.

**Example**: "Fix bug in auth" → Actionable, >2 min → Add to "Dev tasks" list.

---

### 3. Organize

**Goal**: Sort tasks into categories.

**Lists**:
- **Next Actions**: Tasks you can do now (by context: @computer, @home, @calls)
- **Projects**: Multi-step outcomes (e.g., "Ship v2.0")
- **Waiting For**: Blocked on others
- **Someday/Maybe**: Ideas for later

---

### 4. Reflect

**Goal**: Review system regularly.

**Daily**: Check today's tasks (morning planning)
**Weekly**: Review all lists, update projects, plan next week

**Weekly Review Template**:
1. Clear inboxes (email, Slack, notes)
2. Review "Next Actions" (anything stuck?)
3. Review "Projects" (progress? next steps?)
4. Review "Waiting For" (follow up?)
5. Plan next week's priorities

---

### 5. Engage

**Goal**: Do the work.

**How to choose**: Use context (@computer), energy (deep work vs admin), time (15 min vs 2 hours).

---

## Eisenhower Matrix

**Principle**: Prioritize by urgency × importance.

| | **Urgent** | **Not Urgent** |
|---|---|---|
| **Important** | **Q1: Do First** (crises, deadlines) | **Q2: Schedule** (strategy, learning, health) |
| **Not Important** | **Q3: Delegate** (interruptions, busywork) | **Q4: Eliminate** (distractions, timewasters) |

**Goal**: Spend 70%+ time in Q2 (important but not urgent).

**Examples**:
- **Q1**: Production outage, investor meeting tomorrow
- **Q2**: Exercise, strategic planning, skill development
- **Q3**: Most meetings, email replies
- **Q4**: Social media scrolling, gossip

---

## Time Blocking

**Principle**: Assign every hour of the day to a task or theme.

**How**:
1. **Morning**: Deep work (2-4 hours, hardest task)
2. **Midday**: Meetings, email, collaboration
3. **Afternoon**: Admin, planning, light tasks
4. **Evening**: Personal time (exercise, family, rest)

**Example** (founder's calendar):
- 6-7 AM: Exercise
- 7-9 AM: Deep work (product strategy)
- 9-11 AM: Meetings (1:1s, standups)
- 11-12 PM: Email, Slack
- 12-1 PM: Lunch
- 1-3 PM: Deep work (coding, writing)
- 3-4 PM: Admin (expense reports, planning)
- 4-6 PM: Team collaboration (reviews, brainstorms)
- 6 PM+: Personal time

**Key**: Protect deep work blocks (no meetings, no Slack).

---

## Deep Work

**Principle**: Focused, uninterrupted work on cognitively demanding tasks.

**Rules**:
1. **Eliminate distractions**: Close Slack, email, phone (airplane mode)
2. **Time limit**: 90-120 min max (then break)
3. **Single task**: No multitasking
4. **Environment**: Quiet space, headphones, "Do Not Disturb"

**Example**: Writing a spec, coding a feature, financial modeling.

**Shallow work**: Admin, email, meetings (can be multitasked).

---

## Energy Management

**Principle**: Manage energy, not just time.

**4 Dimensions**:

| Dimension | How to Recharge | Example |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| **Physical** | Sleep, exercise, nutrition | 7-8 hours sleep, daily walk |
| **Emotional** | Social connection, gratitude | Dinner with friends, journal |
| **Mental** | Focus breaks, learning | 5-min meditation, read 30 min |
| **Spiritual** | Purpose, reflection, values | Volunteer, long-term vision |

**Key**: You can't work at 100% for 12 hours. Manage energy like a portfolio (invest to recharge).

---

## Procrastination Strategies

**Why we procrastinate**:
1. **Task is unclear**: Break it into 5-min first step
2. **Task is unpleasant**: Do it first (eat the frog)
3. **Too big**: Chunk it (Pomodoro: 25 min sprints)
4. **Fear of failure**: Lower stakes (MVP, rough draft)

**Example**: "Write blog post" feels big → **First step**: "Write 3-sentence outline" (5 min).

---

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| "I don't have time to plan" | Planning saves time (prevents reactive scrambling). |
| "I work better under pressure" | Chronic urgency burns out. Schedule Q2 work. |
| "Multitasking is efficient" | Context-switching kills focus. Single-task deep work. |
| "I'll remember it" | Your brain is for thinking, not storage. Write it down. |

## Red Flags

- You don't have a task list (everything in your head)
- You spend <20% time in Q2 (important but not urgent)
- You check email/Slack every 5 minutes (no deep work)
- You haven't done a weekly review in >1 month (system decays)
- You're working 12-hour days consistently (energy depleted)

## Verification

- [ ] Task capture system set up (inbox for all inputs)
- [ ] Tasks clarified and organized (Next Actions, Projects, Waiting For, Someday/Maybe)
- [ ] Weekly review scheduled (recurring calendar event)
- [ ] Eisenhower Matrix applied (tasks sorted by urgent × important)
- [ ] Time blocking implemented (calendar shows themed blocks)
- [ ] Deep work blocks protected (no meetings during 2-4 hour focus time)
- [ ] Energy management practiced (sleep, exercise, breaks, purpose)
