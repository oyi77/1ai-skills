# Networking

Framework for building and maintaining professional relationships: outreach, events, follow-up, and relationship cultivation.

## When to Use

- Career transitions (job search, promotion)
- Building professional community (industry peers)
- Sales or business development
- Finding mentors, advisors, or investors
- Expanding knowledge (learn from others)
- **When NOT to use**: Transactional networking (asking for favors without relationship), or spamming random people

## Mindset: Give First

**Principle**: Networking is about generosity, not collecting business cards.

**Give first**:
- Offer help before asking for it
- Share useful information (article, intro, advice)
- Celebrate others' wins (congratulate, share)
- Be the connector ("I know someone who can help")

**Mindset shift**:
- ❌ "What can this person do for me?"
- ✅ "How can I be useful to this person?"

## Outreach Templates

### Cold Email (to someone you admire)

> **Subject**: Question about [topic you discuss]  
>   
> Hi [Name],  
>   
> I'm a [role] in [field]. I've been following your work on [specific thing].  
>   
> I'm working on [your project/learning] and would love your perspective on [specific question]. Could I buy you a coffee for 15 minutes?  
>   
> Best,  
> [Your Name]

> **Subject**: Intro: [Name] - [Reason]  
>

>

>   
> I'm [role] working on [thing]. Would love 15 minutes of your time to ask about [specific topic].  
>   
> Best,  
> [Your Name]

>   
> Hi [Name],

>   
> Would love to stay in touch. Let me know if I can ever be helpful.  
>   
> Best,  
> [Your Name]

## Event Strategies

### Before the event

1. **Research**: Who's attending? (speakers list, event app)
2. **Set goals**: "I'll have 3 quality conversations and 1 coffee follow-up"
3. **Prepare**: Your 30-second introduction (who you are, what you do, what you're curious about)

### During the event

- **Arrive early**: Easier to break into small groups
- **Approach groups**: Join a group of 2-3 people (easier than 1 person)
- **Ask open questions**: "What brought you to this event?"
- **Listen more**: 80/20 rule (listen 80%, talk 20%)
- **Exit gracefully**: "It was great meeting you. I'm going to grab another coffee and meet more people. Would love to continue this on email."

### After the event

- **Follow up within 48 hours**: "Great meeting you at [event]. Enjoyed hearing about [topic]."
- **Connect on LinkedIn**: Add a personal note
- **Schedule coffee**: If conversation warrants deeper connection
- **Add to your CRM**: Keep track of contacts (Notion, Airtable, CRM tool)

## Relationship Cultivation

**Frequency**: Every 3-6 months (touch base)

**Types of touch points**:
1. **Share something useful**: Article, job posting, intro
2. **Congratulate**: "Saw you got promoted / launched / won award. Awesome!"
3. **Ask a question**: "What's top of mind in your work right now?"
4. **Offer help**: "Saw you're hiring. Can I help find candidates?"

**Template**:
> Hi [Name],  
>   
> It's been a while. Hope you're well.  
>   
> I came across [article/resource] and thought of you.  
>   
> How are things on your end?  
>   
> Best,  
> [Your Name]

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| "I'm an introvert, I hate networking" | Networking = genuine connection. 1-on-1 coffee > large events. Build in your style. |
| "Networking is transactional" | Only if you make it. Give first, build relationships, never keep score. |
| "I already have enough contacts" | Your network compounds. More diverse connections = more opportunities. |
| "I'll connect when I need something" | Too late. Build before you need it. |

## Red Flags

- You only reach out when you need something (transactional reputation)
- You don't follow up after events (connections evaporate)
- You collect contacts but never cultivate them (hoarding, not networking)
- Your network is all people just like you (no diversity)
- You've never introduced two people in your network (no connector behavior)
