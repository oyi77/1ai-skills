# Executive Presence

Framework for commanding rooms and projecting authority: body language, vocal tonality, storytelling, handling pressure, and credibility signals.

## When to Use

- Board meetings, investor pitches, all-hands
- High-stakes presentations or negotiations
- Leading through crisis or uncertainty
- Building credibility with executives or clients
- **When NOT to use**: 1:1 coaching (use empathy), or peer collaboration (use partnership, not authority)

## The 3 Pillars

### 1. Gravitas (How You Act)

**Principle**: Confidence, composure, decisiveness.

**Signals**:
- **Calm under pressure**: Don't panic visibly
- **Decisive**: Make clear calls ("We're doing X")
- **Owns outcomes**: "I was wrong" or "We succeeded"

**Examples**:
- ❌ "I think maybe we should consider possibly doing X?"
- ✅ "We're doing X. Here's why."

---

### 2. Communication (How You Speak)

**Principle**: Clear, concise, compelling.

**Signals**:
- **Bottom-line-up-front (BLUF)**: Lead with conclusion
- **Simple language**: No jargon or filler
- **Storytelling**: Data + narrative

**Examples**:
- ❌ "So, um, we've been looking at the data, and, like, there are some interesting trends..."
- ✅ "Revenue is up 30%. Here's why and what's next."

---

### 3. Appearance (How You Show Up)

**Principle**: Dress, posture, eye contact signal respect and competence.

**Signals**:
- **Dress appropriately**: Match or exceed room's formality
- **Posture**: Stand tall, shoulders back (expansive, not hunched)
- **Eye contact**: Hold gaze (don't dart or stare at floor)

---

## Body Language

### Power Poses (Before High-Stakes Moments)

**Research**: 2 min in expansive pose → ↑ testosterone, ↓ cortisol (Amy Cuddy).

**Poses**:
- **Wonder Woman**: Hands on hips, chest out
- **Victory**: Arms raised in V
- **Expansive sitting**: Legs spread, arms on armrests

**When**: Before pitch, negotiation, difficult conversation (not during—looks forced).

---

### During the Room

| Signal | Weak | Strong |
|--------|------|--------|
| **Posture** | Hunched, leaning back | Upright, shoulders back |
| **Hands** | Fidgeting, in pockets | Calm, open gestures |
| **Eye contact** | Darting, avoiding | Steady (hold 3-5 sec) |
| **Walking** | Fast, nervous | Purposeful, measured |
| **Space** | Small, apologetic | Take space confidently |

---

## Vocal Tonality

### Key Dimensions

| Dimension | Weak | Strong |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **Volume** | Mumbling | Clear, audible (project) |
| **Pace** | Too fast (nervous) | Measured (pauses for effect) |
| **Pitch** | High (anxious) | Lower register (calm authority) |
| **Filler words** | "Um, uh, like, you know" | Silence instead of filler |

**Practice**: Record yourself. Listen for fillers. Replace with pauses.

---

### Strategic Pauses

**Use silence to emphasize**:
- Before key points: [pause] "Here's what matters."
- After questions: [pause 2 sec, then answer calmly]
- To command room: [pause, make eye contact, then speak]

**Example**: "We have three options. [pause] Only one is viable. [pause] Here's why."

---

## Storytelling Structure

**Framework**: Situation → Complication → Resolution → Lesson

**Example** (pitching a pivot):
1. **Situation**: "We launched product X targeting SMBs."
2. **Complication**: "Retention was 30%. Unsustainable."
3. **Resolution**: "We interviewed 100 churned users. Found enterprise was the real buyer. Pivoted."
4. **Lesson**: "Revenue is now $5M ARR, 90% retention. Listening to customers saved us."

**Key**: Data alone is boring. Story makes it memorable.

---

## Handling Pressure

### When Challenged

**Technique**: Pause → Acknowledge → Respond

**Example**:
- **Challenge**: "Your forecast is way too optimistic."
- ❌ Defensive: "No, it's based on data!"
- ✅ Composed: [pause] "Good question. Let me walk you through the assumptions. If X changes, here's how it adjusts."

---

### When You Don't Know

**Technique**: Admit it confidently, commit to follow-up.

**Example**:
- **Question**: "What's your churn rate by cohort?"
- ❌ Bluffing: "Um, around, like, maybe 3%?"
- ✅ Honest: "I don't have that breakout here. I'll send it by EOD."

**Key**: Confidence includes admitting gaps (shows integrity).

---

## Credibility Signals

| Signal | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Prepared**: Know your numbers, have backup slides | Shows competence |
| **On time**: Arrive early, start/end punctually | Shows respect |
| **Follow through**: Do what you say | Builds trust |
| **Admit mistakes**: "I was wrong" | Shows strength, not weakness |
| **Give credit**: "The team executed this" | Shows leadership |

---

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| "I'm not naturally charismatic" | Executive presence is learnable (practice, feedback, reps). |
| "Being authentic means not changing" | Authenticity ≠ unpolished. You can be genuine AND composed. |
| "Pauses feel awkward" | Silence signals confidence (rushing signals nervousness). |
| "I don't need to dress up" | Appearance is a signal of respect for the room. |

## Red Flags

- You fill every silence with words (no pauses)
- You avoid eye contact (signals low confidence)
- You apologize unnecessarily ("Sorry, just one quick thing...")
- You ramble (no BLUF—bury the lead)
- You get visibly flustered under pressure (no composure)

## Verification

- [ ] Body language assessed (posture, eye contact, gestures)
- [ ] Vocal tonality recorded and reviewed (volume, pace, pitch, fillers)
- [ ] Strategic pauses practiced (before key points, after questions)
- [ ] Storytelling structure used (Situation → Complication → Resolution → Lesson)
- [ ] Pressure handling practiced (pause → acknowledge → respond)
- [ ] Credibility signals present (prepared, on time, admit mistakes, give credit)
