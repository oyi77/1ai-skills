# Partnership Development

## Overview

Mindset skill for partnership development. Builds capability through deliberate practice and structured frameworks.

## When to Use

**Trigger phrases:**
- "partnership development"
- "Building channel partnerships (resellers, affiliates, integrations)"
- "Strategic alliances (co-marketing, co-selling)"
- "Platform ecosystems (marketplace, app store)"


- Building channel partnerships (resellers, affiliates, integrations)
- Strategic alliances (co-marketing, co-selling)
- Platform ecosystems (marketplace, app store)
- Distribution partnerships
- **When NOT to use**: One-off vendor relationships (procurement), or when building in-house capabilities is better


## When NOT to Use

- When the situation requires professional counseling or therapy
- For high-stakes decisions that need expert judgment
- When the skill conflicts with cultural or personal values


## Core Principles

- **Start small** — Begin with manageable partnership development practices and build incrementally
- **Be consistent** — Daily practice beats occasional intense effort
- **Track progress** — Measure improvement to maintain motivation
- **Reflect regularly** — Review what's working and adjust your approach

## Daily Practice

1. **Morning intention** — Set a specific partnership development goal for the day
2. **Active practice** — Apply partnership development techniques during real situations
3. **Evening reflection** — Review the day's partnership development moments and lessons learned
4. **Journal entry** — Record insights, wins, and areas for improvement

## Frameworks

- **OODA Loop** — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — for rapid partnership development decision cycles
- **PDCA Cycle** — Plan, Do, Check, Act — for iterative partnership development improvement
- **After-Action Review** — What happened? Why? What to improve?
- **Deliberate Practice** — Focused effort on specific partnership development weaknesses

## Common Pitfalls

- **Perfectionism** — Waiting for ideal conditions instead of starting now
- **Inconsistency** — Practicing sporadically instead of building a routine
- **Isolation** — Not seeking feedback or accountability partners
- **Overwhelm** — Trying to improve everything at once instead of focusing

## Resources
