# Unit Economics

## Overview

Mindset skill for unit economics. Builds capability through deliberate practice and structured frameworks.

## When to Use

- Evaluating business model sustainability
- Deciding whether to scale go-to-market
- Optimizing pricing, retention, or acquisition
- Investor diligence or board reporting
- **When NOT to use**: Pre-revenue (focus on PMF first), or non-profit models


## When NOT to Use

- When the situation requires professional counseling or therapy
- For high-stakes decisions that need expert judgment
- When the skill conflicts with cultural or personal values


## Core Principles

- **Start small** — Begin with manageable unit economics practices and build incrementally
- **Be consistent** — Daily practice beats occasional intense effort
- **Track progress** — Measure improvement to maintain motivation
- **Reflect regularly** — Review what's working and adjust your approach

## Daily Practice

1. **Morning intention** — Set a specific unit economics goal for the day
2. **Active practice** — Apply unit economics techniques during real situations
3. **Evening reflection** — Review the day's unit economics moments and lessons learned
4. **Journal entry** — Record insights, wins, and areas for improvement

## Frameworks

- **OODA Loop** — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — for rapid unit economics decision cycles
- **PDCA Cycle** — Plan, Do, Check, Act — for iterative unit economics improvement
- **After-Action Review** — What happened? Why? What to improve?
- **Deliberate Practice** — Focused effort on specific unit economics weaknesses

## Common Pitfalls

- **Perfectionism** — Waiting for ideal conditions instead of starting now
- **Inconsistency** — Practicing sporadically instead of building a routine
- **Isolation** — Not seeking feedback or accountability partners
- **Overwhelm** — Trying to improve everything at once instead of focusing
