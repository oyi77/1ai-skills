# Stakeholder Management

Framework for identifying, mapping, engaging, and aligning stakeholders. Covers power-interest grid, RACI matrix, communication plans, and difficult stakeholder strategies.

## When to Use

- Managing complex projects with multiple stakeholders
- Navigating organizational politics or cross-functional initiatives
- Getting buy-in for major decisions
- Aligning executives, teams, and external partners
- **When NOT to use**: Simple projects with 1-2 stakeholders (1:1 communication is sufficient)

## Stakeholder Mapping

### Power-Interest Grid

| | Low Interest | High Interest |
|---|---|---|
| **High Power** | **Keep Satisfied** (manage influence) | **Manage Closely** (key stakeholder) |
| **Low Power** | **Monitor** (minimal effort) | **Keep Informed** (champion) |

**Example (new product launch)**:

| Stakeholder | Power | Interest | Strategy |
|-------------|-------|----------|----------|
| CEO | High | High | **Manage closely**: Weekly update, strategic alignment |
| VP Eng | High | Low | **Keep satisfied**: Monthly update, only major decisions |
| Product Manager | Low | High | **Keep informed**: Weekly sync, detailed status |
| Intern | Low | Low | **Monitor**: Public updates only |

---

### Stakeholder Salience Model (Mitchell et al.)

**3 dimensions**:
1. **Power**: Can they influence outcomes?
2. **Legitimacy**: Is their involvement appropriate?
3. **Urgency**: How time-sensitive are their demands?

**Prioritize**: High on all 3 = definitive stakeholder (top priority).

## RACI Matrix (Who Does What)

| Activity | Person A (Sponsor) | Person B (PM) | Person C (Team) | Person D (QA) |
|----------|-------------------|----------------|------------------|----------------|
| Define requirements | A | R | C | I |
| Build feature | I | A | R | C |
| Test | I | A | C | R |
| Deploy | I | R | A | I |
| Communicate | A | R | I | I |

**Key**:
- **R** = Responsible (does the work)
- **A** = Accountable (the buck stops here—only one per task)
- **C** = Consulted (input before decision)
- **I** = Informed (told after decision)

## Communication Plan

| Stakeholder | Frequency | Channel | Content | Owner |
|-------------|-----------|---------|---------|-------|
| Exec sponsor | Weekly | 1:1 (30min) | Progress, risks, decisions needed | PM |
| Project team | Daily | Standup (15min) | Blockers, progress, next steps | PM |
| Cross-functional leads | Biweekly | Sync (1hr) | Status, alignment, dependencies | PM |
| All employees | Monthly | Email/newsletter | High-level progress, milestones | Comms lead |

## Difficult Stakeholder Strategies

| Type | Behavior | Strategy |
|------|----------|----------|
| **The Critic** | Always finds problems | Ask for solutions: "What would you suggest?" |
| **The Hoarder** | Withholds information | Build trust: Share first, they'll reciprocate. |
| **The Bystander** | Says nothing, passively blocks | Direct ask: "Do you agree with this approach?" |
| **The Champion** | Enthusiastic but overcommits | Constrain scope: "Let's start with X and expand." |
| **The Executive** | Skips details, wants summaries | Bottom-line-up-front: 1-page executive summary |

**General approach**:
1. **Understand their motivation**: Why do they act this way? (fear, power, workload)
2. **Find common ground**: What do we both want?
3. **Communicate in their language**: Execs: outcomes. Engineers: details. Sales: revenue.
4. **Build trust over time**: Consistent, transparent communication

## Stakeholder Mapping (Salience Model)

**Dimensions**:
- **Power** (can they force action?)
- **Legitimacy** (is their involvement appropriate?)
- **Urgency** (how time-critical?)

**Types**:
- **Definitive**: All 3 dimensions (top priority)
- **Dominant**: Power + Legitimacy (needs management)
- **Dangerous**: Power + Urgency (threat if not managed)
- **Dependent**: Legitimacy + Urgency (needs advocate)
- **Dormant**: Power only (potential stakeholder)

**Decision**: Focus on Definitive > Dominant > Dependent, in that order.

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| "I don't have time to manage stakeholders" | Stakeholder management IS the work. Ignoring them = project risk. |
| "Everyone's on the same page" | They're never on the same page unless you actively manage alignment. |
| "I'll just CC everyone" | Over-communication without structure = noise. Tailor by stakeholder. |
| "They'll come to me if they have concerns" | Silent stakeholders are often the most dangerous (passive blocking). |

## Red Flags

- You don't know who your stakeholders are (no map)
- You've never asked a stakeholder what they care about
- You send the same update to everyone (no tailoring)
- You find out about stakeholder concerns in meetings (should have known beforehand)
- A stakeholder goes silent and then blocks your project (passive resistance)

## Verification

- [ ] All stakeholders identified and mapped (power-interest grid, 2+ dimensions)
- [ ] RACI matrix completed (R/A for each major activity)
- [ ] Communication plan defined (frequency, channel, content per stakeholder)
- [ ] Key stakeholders pre-briefed before major meetings
- [ ] Difficult stakeholder strategies ready (critic, hoarder, bystander, executive)
- [ ] Stakeholder stance tracked (current vs desired, with action plan to close gap)
