# First Principles Thinking

## Overview

Mindset skill for first principles thinking. Builds capability through deliberate practice and structured frameworks.

## When to Use

- Solving novel problems with no precedent
- Challenging industry assumptions or "best practices"
- Innovating in constrained environments (cost, time, resources)
- Questioning why things are done a certain way
- **When NOT to use**: Routine problems with proven solutions, tight deadlines requiring fast execution, or when reinventing the wheel wastes time


## When NOT to Use

- When the situation requires professional counseling or therapy
- For high-stakes decisions that need expert judgment
- When the skill conflicts with cultural or personal values


## Core Principles

- **Start small** — Begin with manageable first principles thinking practices and build incrementally
- **Be consistent** — Daily practice beats occasional intense effort
- **Track progress** — Measure improvement to maintain motivation
- **Reflect regularly** — Review what's working and adjust your approach

## Daily Practice

1. **Morning intention** — Set a specific first principles thinking goal for the day
2. **Active practice** — Apply first principles thinking techniques during real situations
3. **Evening reflection** — Review the day's first principles thinking moments and lessons learned
4. **Journal entry** — Record insights, wins, and areas for improvement

## Frameworks

- **OODA Loop** — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — for rapid first principles thinking decision cycles
- **PDCA Cycle** — Plan, Do, Check, Act — for iterative first principles thinking improvement
- **After-Action Review** — What happened? Why? What to improve?
- **Deliberate Practice** — Focused effort on specific first principles thinking weaknesses

## Common Pitfalls

- **Perfectionism** — Waiting for ideal conditions instead of starting now
- **Inconsistency** — Practicing sporadically instead of building a routine
- **Isolation** — Not seeking feedback or accountability partners
- **Overwhelm** — Trying to improve everything at once instead of focusing

## Resources

- Keep a first principles thinking journal for tracking progress
- Find an accountability partner or mentor
- Set weekly first principles thinking challenges with measurable outcomes
- Review and adjust your approach monthly
