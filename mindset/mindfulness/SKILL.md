# Mindfulness

Framework for practicing mindfulness: meditation, breathing techniques, body scans, mindful communication, and integrating presence into daily life.

## When to Use

- Reducing stress and anxiety
- Improving focus and concentration
- Enhancing emotional regulation
- Building self-awareness
- **When NOT to use**: Acute crisis (use crisis management), or clinical depression (refer to professional)

## Core Practices

### 1. Breath Awareness (Foundation)

**Technique**:
1. Sit comfortably, close eyes
2. Focus on breath entering and leaving nostrils
3. When mind wanders (it will), gently bring attention back to breath
4. Start with 5 minutes, work up to 20

**Why**: Trains attention. Builds "mental muscle" for focus.

---

### 2. Body Scan

**Technique**:
1. Lie down or sit
2. Bring attention to feet (5 seconds)
3. Slowly move up: ankles, calves, knees, thighs, hips
4. Continue up through torso, arms, neck, head
5. Notice sensations (warmth, tension, numbness) without judgment

**Duration**: 10-30 minutes.

**Benefits**: Reduces physical tension. Improves mind-body connection.

---

### 3. Loving-Kindness (Metta)

**Phrases** (repeat silently):
- "May I be happy"
- "May I be healthy"
- "May I be safe"
- "May I live with ease"

**Expansion**:
1. Yourself
2. Someone you love
3. A neutral person
4. Someone difficult
5. All beings

**Benefits**: Builds compassion, reduces resentment.

---

### 4. Mindful Walking

**Technique**:
1. Walk slowly (or normal pace)
2. Focus on sensations: feet touching ground, air on skin, sounds
3. When mind wanders, return to physical sensation

**Duration**: 10-20 minutes.

## Integrating Mindfulness into Daily Life

| Activity | How to Practice |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Waking up** | 5 breaths before getting out of bed |
| **Brushing teeth** | Feel the brush, taste the toothpaste, hear the water |
| **Eating** | First 5 bites without phone/TV. Taste fully. |
| **Walking** | Feel feet on ground, air on skin |
| **Listening** | 100% attention, no planning response |
| **Before meetings** | Take 3 deep breaths before joining |
| **Stress trigger** | Pause. 1 breath. Respond don't react. |

---

## Red Flags

- You can't sit with your own thoughts for 2 minutes without distraction
- You react impulsively and regret it (low emotional regulation)
- You're always "on" (no mental breaks)
- You're disconnected from physical sensations (ignore hunger, fatigue, pain)
- You find yourself ruminating (replaying past, worrying about future)

---

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| "I don't have time to meditate" | 5 minutes = better than 0. You have 5 minutes. |
| "My mind is too busy to meditate" | That's exactly why you should. The goal isn't empty mind—it's noticing the busyness. |
| "I tried once, it didn't work" | Mindfulness is a skill. Practice, not perfection. |
| "Sitting still feels unproductive" | Mindfulness increases productivity (focus, clarity, less reactivity). |

## Verification

- [ ] Daily meditation started (5+ minutes, 7 days)
- [ ] Body scan practiced (10+ minutes, weekly)
- [ ] Mindful moments integrated into daily activities (eating, walking, listening)
- [ ] Breath awareness used before stressful situations (3 breaths)
- [ ] One mindfulness session completed without judgment of "doing it wrong"
