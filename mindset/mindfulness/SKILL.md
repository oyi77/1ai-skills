# Mindfulness

## When to Use

- Reducing stress and anxiety
- Improving focus and concentration
- Enhancing emotional regulation
- Building self-awareness
- **When NOT to use**: Acute crisis (use crisis management), or clinical depression (refer to professional)

## Core Principles

- **Start small** — Begin with manageable mindfulness practices and build incrementally
- **Be consistent** — Daily practice beats occasional intense effort
- **Track progress** — Measure improvement to maintain motivation
- **Reflect regularly** — Review what's working and adjust your approach

## Daily Practice

1. **Morning intention** — Set a specific mindfulness goal for the day
2. **Active practice** — Apply mindfulness techniques during real situations
3. **Evening reflection** — Review the day's mindfulness moments and lessons learned
4. **Journal entry** — Record insights, wins, and areas for improvement

## Frameworks

- **OODA Loop** — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — for rapid mindfulness decision cycles
- **PDCA Cycle** — Plan, Do, Check, Act — for iterative mindfulness improvement
- **After-Action Review** — What happened? Why? What to improve?
- **Deliberate Practice** — Focused effort on specific mindfulness weaknesses

## Common Pitfalls

- **Perfectionism** — Waiting for ideal conditions instead of starting now
- **Inconsistency** — Practicing sporadically instead of building a routine
- **Isolation** — Not seeking feedback or accountability partners
- **Overwhelm** — Trying to improve everything at once instead of focusing

## Resources

- Keep a mindfulness journal for tracking progress
- Find an accountability partner or mentor
- Set weekly mindfulness challenges with measurable outcomes
- Review and adjust your approach monthly
