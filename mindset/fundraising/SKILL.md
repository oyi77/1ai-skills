# Fundraising

Framework for raising capital from angel investors and VCs. Covers pitch deck structure, term sheet basics, investor outreach, and runway management.

## When to Use

- Raising seed, Series A, or later-stage capital
- Preparing for investor meetings or pitch competitions
- Evaluating term sheets or negotiating terms
- Building investor pipeline and relationships
- **When NOT to use**: Pre-product or pre-PMF (focus on customers first), or when bootstrapping is viable

## The 10-Slide Pitch Deck

**Rule**: 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30-point font minimum (Guy Kawasaki).

### Slide 1: Problem

**Purpose**: Show the pain is real, urgent, and widespread.

**Structure**: 1-2 sentences + stat
- "40% of SaaS startups fail because they can't collect payments."
- "$12B lost annually to billing infrastructure complexity."

---

### Slide 2: Solution

**Purpose**: Show your product solves the problem elegantly.

**Structure**: Product screenshot + one sentence
- "Accept payments in 10 lines of code."

---

### Slide 3: Business Model

**Purpose**: How do you make money?

**Structure**: Revenue model + unit economics
- "2.9% + 30¢ per transaction"
- "$100K ARR after 6 months"

---

### Slide 4: Market Size

**Purpose**: Show the opportunity is big enough.

**Structure**: TAM / SAM / SOM
- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market): $200B global payments
- **SAM** (Serviceable Available Market): $50B online SaaS payments
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market): $500M (1% of SAM)

**Benchmark**: VCs want $1B+ TAM.

---

### Slide 5: Competition

**Purpose**: Show you understand the landscape and have differentiation.

**Structure**: 2×2 matrix or table
- X-axis: Developer-friendly vs Enterprise
- Y-axis: Pricing (low vs high)
- Place competitors and yourself

**Key**: Don't say "no competitors" (that's naive). Show why you win.

---

### Slide 6: Traction

**Purpose**: Prove customers want this.

**Structure**: Key metrics + growth chart
- "500 customers, $2M ARR, 3x YoY growth"
- Revenue/user/MRR chart going up and to the right

**Pre-revenue?** Show user growth, engagement, or LOIs (letters of intent).

---

### Slide 7: Team

**Purpose**: Why are you uniquely positioned to win?

**Structure**: Photos + 1-line bios
- "Jane (CEO): Ex-Stripe, built payments at scale"
- "John (CTO): PhD in cryptography, 2 patents"

**Key**: Highlight domain expertise and past wins.

---

### Slide 8: Financials

**Purpose**: Show you have a plan and understand unit economics.

**Structure**: 3-year projection (revenue, burn, headcount)
- Year 1: $1M ARR, $2M burn, 15 people
- Year 2: $5M ARR, $4M burn, 40 people
- Year 3: $15M ARR, break-even, 80 people

---

### Slide 9: Use of Funds

**Purpose**: What will you do with the money?

**Structure**: Pie chart or bar chart
- 50% Engineering (product development)
- 30% Sales & Marketing (growth)
- 20% Operations (infrastructure, support)

**Key**: Show how capital accelerates growth, not just extends runway.

---

### Slide 10: Closing (Vision)

**Purpose**: Leave them inspired.

**Structure**: Big vision + ask
- "We're building the payment infrastructure for the next million SaaS companies."
- "Raising $5M Series A. Join us."

---

## The Pitch Narrative Arc

**Structure**: Problem → Solution → Proof → Vision → Ask

**Example**:
1. **Hook**: "Billing is broken. 40% of SaaS startups fail because of it."
2. **Tension**: "Stripe is too complex. PayPal takes 10%. Homegrown systems break."
3. **Solution**: "We built billing that scales from $0 to $100M ARR in 10 lines of code."
4. **Proof**: "500 companies, $2M ARR, 3x YoY growth."
5. **Vision**: "We're the payment layer for the next generation of SaaS."
6. **Ask**: "Raising $5M. Will you join us?"

## Term Sheet Basics

### Key Terms

| Term | What It Means | Negotiable? |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| **Valuation (pre-money)** | Company value before investment | Yes (highly negotiated) |
| **Equity %** | How much of company investors own | Yes (tied to valuation) |
| **Liquidation preference** | Who gets paid first in exit (1x = fair, >1x = investor-favored) | Yes |
| **Board seats** | How many board seats investors get | Yes |
| **Pro-rata rights** | Investor can maintain % in future rounds | Standard (usually yes) |
| **Vesting (founders)** | Founders' equity vests over time (4yr with 1yr cliff = standard) | Standard |
| **Anti-dilution** | Protects investors if you raise at lower valuation (full ratchet = bad, weighted average = fair) | Yes |

### SAFE Notes

**Concept**: Simple Agreement for Future Equity (YC standard)

**How it works**: Investor gives cash now, converts to equity at next priced round.

**Key terms**:
- **Valuation cap**: Max valuation at conversion (e.g., $10M cap)
- **Discount**: % discount on next round price (e.g., 20%)

**Example**: $10M cap, $500K investment. Next round at $20M → Investor converts at $10M (gets 5% instead of 2.5%).

## Investor Outreach

**Process**:
1. **Build list**: Target 50-100 investors (stage-appropriate, sector-focused)
2. **Warm intros**: 10x better than cold emails. Ask founders, advisors, or angels to intro
3. **Send deck**: After intro, send 1-page summary + deck
4. **First meeting**: Pitch, Q&A, gauge interest
5. **Follow-up**: Data room, diligence, term sheet

**Timeline**: 3-6 months for seed/Series A.

---

## Runway Management

**Formula**: `Runway (months) = Cash / Monthly Burn`

**Rule**: Raise when you have 6-9 months runway left (not 3 months—too late).

**Example**:
- Cash: $500K
- Burn: $50K/month
- Runway: 10 months → **Start fundraising now**

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| "We'll raise when we need it" | Fundraising takes 3-6 months. Start early. |
| "Valuation is everything" | Terms matter (liquidation preference, board control). Read the fine print. |
| "We just need one yes" | You need multiple term sheets to negotiate leverage. |
| "Investors will figure out the value" | You must tell the story. They invest in narrative + data. |

## Red Flags

- You don't have traction metrics (revenue, users, engagement)
- You can't answer "What's your burn rate?" or "How long is your runway?"
- You're raising "because competitors are"
- Your pitch is >20 minutes or >10 slides
- You say "no competitors" or "we have no competition"

## Verification

- [ ] 10-slide pitch deck complete (problem, solution, business model, market, competition, traction, team, financials, use of funds, closing)
- [ ] Traction metrics quantified (revenue, users, growth rate)
- [ ] Target investor list built (50-100 investors, stage-appropriate)
- [ ] Warm intro paths identified (who can introduce you?)
- [ ] Runway calculated (months of cash remaining)
- [ ] Term sheet basics understood (valuation, liquidation preference, board seats, anti-dilution)
