# Habit Formation

Framework for building and maintaining habits: cue-routine-reward loop, habit stacking, environment design, and breaking bad habits.

## When to Use

- Building new routines (exercise, reading, learning)
- Breaking bad habits (procrastination, mindless scrolling)
- Improving productivity or health
- Sustaining changes long-term (beyond 21 days)
- **When NOT to use**: One-time actions (use task lists), or clinical issues (addiction treatment)

## The Habit Loop (Cue → Routine → Reward)

### Cue

**Trigger** that starts the habit.

**Types**:
- **Time**: "At 7 AM I..."
- **Location**: "When I enter the gym..."
- **Emotion**: "When I feel stressed..."
- **Preceding action**: "After I brush my teeth..."
- **Other people**: "When my coworker offers coffee..."

**Design your cues**:
- Make them obvious! Put your running shoes by the door.
- Choose consistent triggers (same time, same place).

---

### Routine

**Action** you take (the habit itself).

**Design**:
- Start small (2-minute rule: "run for 2 minutes", "write 1 sentence")
- Make it easy (reduce friction)
- Remove obstacles (lay out gym clothes the night before)

---

### Reward

**Benefit** you get (signals success to your brain).

**Types**:
- **Immediate**: "I feel good after a run" (endorphins)
- **Tangible**: "I get a coffee after the gym"
- **Tracking**: "I check off my streak (dopamine from completion)"

**Key**: Reward must be immediate (dopamine is short-lived).

---

## Habit Stacking

**Formula**: "After [existing habit], I will [new habit]."

**Example**:
- "After I pour my morning coffee, I will meditate for 1 minute."
- "After I finish dinner, I will write 3 things I'm grateful for."
- "After I get into bed, I will read 10 pages."

**Why it works**: Existing habit = built-in cue.

## Habit Scorecard

**Track for one week**:

| Time | Cue | Action | Automatic? | Keep? |
|------|-----|--------|------------|-------|
| 7:00 AM | Alarm | Check phone | Yes | Replace with reading |
| 7:30 AM | Coffee | Read news | Yes | Keep |
| 9:00 AM | Email | Open Slack | Yes | Delay to 10 AM |

**Purpose**: Identify habits you want to keep, replace, or stop.

## Breaking Bad Habits

**Inversion of the 4 laws** (Clear):

| Good Habit | Bad Habit (Inverse) |
|------------|---------------------|
| Make it obvious | Make it invisible |
| Make it attractive | Make it unattractive |
| Make it easy | Make it difficult |
| Make it satisfying | Make it unsatisfying |

**Example** (breaking mindless scrolling):
- **Make it invisible**: Delete social media apps from phone
- **Make it unattractive**: Remind yourself "this is stealing my time"
- **Make it difficult**: 20-second delay before opening (log out)
- **Make it unsatisfying**: Set a timer and screen time limit

---

## Environment Design

**Principle**: Make good habits easy, bad habits hard.

**Good habit design**:
- Put gym clothes on the nightstand (visible)
- Pre-schedule workouts (calendar)
- Lay out workspace before bed (reduce friction)

**Bad habit prevention**:
- Leave phone in another room at night
- Keep junk food out of the house
- Disable notifications for distracting apps

---

## Streaks and Accountability

**Streaks**: Track consecutive days (builds momentum).

**Accountability**: Tell someone your goal. "I will run 3x this week."

**Technology**: Habit tracking apps (Streaks, Habitica, etc.).

---

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| "I need motivation to start" | Action creates motivation, not the other way around. Just start. |
| "I need to do it perfectly" | "Never miss twice." It's okay to miss a day. Just get back on track. |
| "21 days to form a habit" | Average is 66 days (range 18-254). Be patient. |
| "I need willpower" | Willpower is finite. Environment design beats willpower. |

## Red Flags

- You rely on motivation (inconsistent)
- You try to change too many habits at once (overwhelming)
- You don't track (no feedback loop)
- Your environment works against you (phone in bedroom, junk food visible)
- You stop after one missed day (perfectionism)

## Verification

- [ ] Habit loop designed: cue (obvious) → routine (easy) → reward (immediate)
- [ ] Habit stacking: attached new habit to existing one
- [ ] Environment designed: reduce friction for good, increase for bad
- [ ] 2-minute rule applied: "make it so easy you can't say no"
- [ ] Tracking method set (streak counter, app, or journal)
- [ ] Accountability in place (told someone your commitment)
- [ ] "Never miss twice" principle adopted
