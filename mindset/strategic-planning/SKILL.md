# Strategic Planning

Framework for setting and executing strategy: OKRs, roadmaps, quarterly planning, execution tracking, and review cadence.

## When to Use

- Setting annual or quarterly strategic direction
- Aligning teams on priorities
- Tracking execution and progress
- Adjusting strategy based on results
- **When NOT to use**: Tactical day-to-day execution (use sprint planning), or highly uncertain environments (use experiments instead)

## OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)

**Structure**: Objective (qualitative) + 3-5 Key Results (quantitative).

**Objective**: Inspirational goal (WHERE we're going)
**Key Results**: Measurable outcomes (HOW we know we got there)

**Example**:
- **Objective**: Become the go-to platform for mid-market SaaS companies
- **KR1**: Achieve $10M ARR (from $5M)
- **KR2**: Reach 500 customers (from 250)
- **KR3**: Increase NPS to 60 (from 45)

---

### OKR Writing Guide

**Good Objectives**:
- Inspirational and memorable
- Directional (not just numbers)
- Time-bound (quarterly or annual)

**Good Key Results**:
- Measurable (number, %, $)
- Outcome-focused (not activity: "Ship 10 features" is bad; "Increase retention 20%" is good)
- Ambitious but achievable (70% confidence you'll hit it)

**Bad OKR**:
- **Objective**: Improve the product
- **KR1**: Ship 5 features

**Why bad?** Objective is vague. KR is activity, not outcome.

**Good OKR**:
- **Objective**: Deliver world-class user experience
- **KR1**: Reduce page load time to <1 second (from 3s)
- **KR2**: Increase weekly active users 30% (from 10K to 13K)
- **KR3**: NPS increases to 70 (from 55)

---

### OKR Scoring

**Scale**: 0.0 to 1.0

- **0.0-0.3**: Missed (red flag)
- **0.4-0.6**: Partial success (expected)
- **0.7-1.0**: Exceeded (maybe too easy next time)

**Example**:
- KR1: Achieve $10M ARR → Actual $8M ARR → **Score: 0.6** (60% of goal)
- KR2: 500 customers → Actual 550 customers → **Score: 1.0** (exceeded)
- KR3: NPS 60 → Actual 58 → **Score: 0.87** (87% of improvement)

**Overall OKR score**: Average of KRs = **(0.6 + 1.0 + 0.87) / 3 = 0.82**

## Quarterly Planning

**Cadence**: Plan every quarter (Q1-Q4).

**Process**:
1. **Review last quarter**: What worked? What didn't? (Retrospective)
2. **Set OKRs**: 3-5 company OKRs, each team derives 3-5 team OKRs
3. **Roadmap**: What initiatives support OKRs?
4. **Resource allocation**: Budget, headcount, time
5. **Communicate**: All-hands to share plan

**Timeline**: Plan in last 2 weeks of quarter; start executing Week 1 of new quarter.

---

## Roadmap Structure

**Three horizons**:

| Horizon | Timeframe | Focus | Example |
|---------|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Now** | This quarter | Execution | Ship feature X, close 50 customers |
| **Next** | Next 2 quarters | Validated bets | Explore new segment, pilot integration |
| **Later** | 6-12 months | Strategic bets | International expansion, platform play |

**Key**: "Now" is committed (must ship). "Next" and "Later" are directional (can change).

---

## Strategy Review Cadence

| Meeting | Frequency | Purpose | Attendees |
|---------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Weekly team sync** | Weekly | Tactical blockers, quick updates | Team |
| **Monthly business review** | Monthly | Metrics review, course corrections | Leadership |
| **Quarterly planning** | Quarterly | Set OKRs, roadmap, resource allocation | All company |
| **Annual offsite** | Annually | Long-term strategy, vision, culture | Leadership + key ICs |

---

## Execution Tracking

**Metrics dashboard**:
- OKR progress (updated weekly)
- Key metrics (revenue, users, NPS, churn)
- Leading indicators (pipeline, signups, engagement)

**Status**:
- 🟢 **Green**: On track
- 🟡 **Yellow**: At risk (needs attention)
- 🔴 **Red**: Off track (needs escalation)

**Example** (weekly update):
- KR1: $10M ARR → Current $7M → 🟡 Yellow (behind pace, need to accelerate)
- KR2: 500 customers → Current 480 → 🟢 Green (on track)
- KR3: NPS 60 → Current 52 → 🔴 Red (not improving fast enough)

---

## Course Correction

**When to pivot**:
- Key Result is 🔴 Red for 4+ weeks (not recovering)
- Market conditions changed (competitor launched, regulation, recession)
- Customer feedback invalidated assumptions

**How to pivot**:
1. **Diagnose**: Why are we off track? (Bad execution? Wrong strategy? External shock?)
2. **Options**: What are alternatives? (Double down? Pivot? Cut losses?)
3. **Decide**: Use decision frameworks (RICE, pre-mortem)
4. **Communicate**: Explain why we're changing (transparency builds trust)
5. **Execute**: Reallocate resources, update OKRs

**Key**: Don't change OKRs lightly (undermines commitment), but don't cling to bad strategy either.

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| "We don't have time to plan" | Planning saves time (avoids misalignment, rework). |
| "OKRs are just paperwork" | OKRs force clarity on priorities. Without them, teams optimize locally. |
| "We'll adjust strategy as we go" | Continuous pivoting = no strategy. Plan, commit, course-correct quarterly. |
| "We hit 100% of our OKRs" | OKRs should be ambitious. 100% = you sandbagged. |

## Red Flags

- You haven't reviewed OKRs in >1 month (flying blind)
- Teams don't know the company OKRs (misalignment)
- You're changing OKRs mid-quarter (lack of commitment)
- OKRs are activity-based ("Ship 10 features") not outcome-based
- You have >5 OKRs per level (lack of focus)

## Verification

- [ ] OKRs set (3-5 company OKRs, each with 3-5 measurable Key Results)
- [ ] Team OKRs derived from company OKRs (cascaded, not siloed)
- [ ] Roadmap defined (Now / Next / Later initiatives mapped to OKRs)
- [ ] Review cadence established (weekly team sync, monthly business review, quarterly planning)
- [ ] Metrics dashboard live (OKR progress tracked weekly, status: green/yellow/red)
- [ ] Last quarter retrospective completed (what worked, what didn't)
