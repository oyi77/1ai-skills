# Hiring Playbook

## When to Use

- Hiring for any role (IC, manager, executive)
- Scaling a team rapidly
- Improving hiring quality or reducing mis-hires
- Onboarding new hires effectively
- **When NOT to use**: Filling roles via internal promotion (different process)

## Core Principles

- **Start small** — Begin with manageable hiring playbook practices and build incrementally
- **Be consistent** — Daily practice beats occasional intense effort
- **Track progress** — Measure improvement to maintain motivation
- **Reflect regularly** — Review what's working and adjust your approach

## Daily Practice

1. **Morning intention** — Set a specific hiring playbook goal for the day
2. **Active practice** — Apply hiring playbook techniques during real situations
3. **Evening reflection** — Review the day's hiring playbook moments and lessons learned
4. **Journal entry** — Record insights, wins, and areas for improvement

## Frameworks

- **OODA Loop** — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — for rapid hiring playbook decision cycles
- **PDCA Cycle** — Plan, Do, Check, Act — for iterative hiring playbook improvement
- **After-Action Review** — What happened? Why? What to improve?
- **Deliberate Practice** — Focused effort on specific hiring playbook weaknesses

## Common Pitfalls

- **Perfectionism** — Waiting for ideal conditions instead of starting now
- **Inconsistency** — Practicing sporadically instead of building a routine
- **Isolation** — Not seeking feedback or accountability partners
- **Overwhelm** — Trying to improve everything at once instead of focusing

## Resources

- Keep a hiring playbook journal for tracking progress
- Find an accountability partner or mentor
- Set weekly hiring playbook challenges with measurable outcomes
- Review and adjust your approach monthly
