# Team Management

Framework for managing teams: 1:1 structure, feedback delivery (SBI model), delegation (70% rule), performance reviews, and team health.

## When to Use

- Managing individual contributors or managers
- Giving feedback (positive or critical)
- Delegating work effectively
- Conducting performance reviews
- Diagnosing team health or morale issues
- **When NOT to use**: Coaching peers (use influence, not authority), or managing up (different skillset)

## 1:1 Meeting Structure

**Frequency**: Weekly for ICs, biweekly for managers

**Duration**: 30-60 minutes

**Template** (their agenda, not yours):
1. **Their topics** (15-20 min): What's on their mind?
2. **Project updates** (10 min): Progress, blockers, help needed
3. **Feedback** (5-10 min): Positive + constructive
4. **Career development** (5-10 min): Growth, learning, goals

**Key principles**:
- **Their meeting**: They drive the agenda
- **Consistent**: Don't cancel (signals they're not a priority)
- **Safe space**: Encourage candor, don't punish honesty

**Questions**:
- "What's top of mind for you this week?"
- "Where are you stuck?"
- "What can I do to help?"
- "What's one thing you'd like to improve at?"

## Feedback: SBI Model

**Structure**: Situation → Behavior → Impact

**Example** (positive):
- **Situation**: "In yesterday's sprint planning..."
- **Behavior**: "...you asked clarifying questions that uncovered a major edge case."
- **Impact**: "That saved us 2 weeks of rework. Great job."

**Example** (constructive):
- **Situation**: "In the client demo Friday..."
- **Behavior**: "...you interrupted the client twice while they were describing their workflow."
- **Impact**: "They went quiet and we didn't learn their real pain points. Can we adjust that?"

**Why SBI?**
- Specific (not vague: "good job")
- Behavioral (observable, not judgmental: "you're rude")
- Tied to outcomes (impact is clear)

## Delegation: The 70% Rule

**Principle**: Delegate when someone can do it 70% as well as you.

**Why?** You free up your time for higher-leverage work. They learn and grow.

**Process**:
1. **Assess**: Can they do this 70%+ as well as me?
2. **Delegate**: Clearly define outcome, not process
3. **Support**: Check in, unblock, but don't micromanage
4. **Review**: Debrief after completion (what went well, what to improve)

**Template**:
> "I'd like you to own [task]. The outcome I'm looking for is [specific result]. Here's context: [background]. Let me know if you hit blockers, but you own the decisions. Make sense?"

**What NOT to delegate**:
- Confidential matters (comp, layoffs, legal)
- High-stakes decisions (customer escalations, strategic pivots) until they're ready

## Delegation Matrix (Situational Leadership)

| Skill | Willingness | Leadership Style | What To Do |
|-------|-------------|------------------|------------|
| Low | Low | **Directing** | Tell them what and how (step-by-step) |
| Low | High | **Coaching** | Explain why, involve them in how |
| High | Low | **Supporting** | Ask why they're hesitant, encourage |
| High | High | **Delegating** | Give outcome, let them own it (70% rule) |

**Key**: Match style to person's readiness (skill + will).

## Performance Reviews

**Frequency**: Quarterly or biannually

**Components**:
1. **Self-review**: What did I achieve? Where did I struggle?
2. **Manager review**: Aligned to goals, with examples
3. **360 feedback**: Input from peers, cross-functional partners
4. **Goals for next period**: 3-5 SMART goals

**Structure**:
- **Achievements**: What went well (specific examples)
- **Growth areas**: What to improve (SBI examples)
- **Goals**: Next quarter/half OKRs
- **Career development**: Skills to build, stretch projects

**Rating scale** (if needed):
- **Exceeds**: Consistently delivers above expectations
- **Meets**: Consistently delivers on expectations
- **Developing**: Partially meets expectations (needs improvement)
- **Does not meet**: Fails to meet expectations (PIP territory)

---

## Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

**When**: Employee is underperforming (rating: "does not meet")

**Purpose**: Clear expectations + support to improve OR documentation for exit.

**Structure**:
1. **Specific gaps**: What's not working (SBI examples)
2. **Expectations**: What "good" looks like
3. **Support**: Resources, coaching, check-ins
4. **Timeline**: 30-90 days
5. **Consequences**: "If not improved, we'll need to part ways"

**Key**: PIPs are serious. Don't sugarcoat. Most people don't come off PIPs (be honest about that).

## Team Health Check

**Metrics**:
- **Engagement**: Are people motivated? (Pulse surveys, 1:1s)
- **Retention**: Low turnover = healthy team
- **Velocity**: Are we shipping? (Sprint velocity, cycle time)
- **Conflict**: Healthy debate or toxic tension?

**Questions** (team retro):
- "What's working well?"
- "What's frustrating us?"
- "What should we start/stop/continue?"

**Red flags**:
- People leaving (especially top performers)
- Low energy in meetings (everyone quiet, no debate)
- Missed deadlines consistently
- Lack of ownership (everyone waits for you to decide)

## Common Rationalizations

| Rationalization | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| "I don't have time for 1:1s" | 1:1s ARE your job. Skip them, lose your best people. |
| "They know what they did wrong" | Unspoken feedback is unfair. Give it explicitly. |
| "I'm faster if I just do it myself" | True short-term, but you'll never scale. Delegate to grow them (and free yourself). |
| "Performance reviews are HR busywork" | Reviews force clarity on expectations and growth. Skip them, expect confusion. |

## Red Flags

- You cancel 1:1s regularly (signals they're not a priority)
- You've never given constructive feedback (they don't know what to improve)
- You delegate tasks but not outcomes (micromanaging)
- You haven't done performance reviews in 12+ months
- Team member is surprised by negative review (feedback wasn't ongoing)

## Verification

- [ ] 1:1s scheduled consistently (weekly for ICs, biweekly for managers)
- [ ] Feedback given using SBI model (Situation-Behavior-Impact)
- [ ] Delegation uses 70% rule (not waiting for 100% readiness)
- [ ] Performance reviews completed on time (quarterly or biannual)
- [ ] Team health metrics tracked (engagement, retention, velocity)
- [ ] Career development discussed in 1:1s (not just project updates)
