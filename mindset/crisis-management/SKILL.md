# Crisis Management

## Overview

Mindset skill for crisis management. Builds capability through deliberate practice and structured frameworks.

## When to Use
**Trigger phrases:**
- "crisis management"
- "Respond to crises using incident command structure, stakeholder communication, a"


- Security breaches or data leaks
- PR disasters or reputation threats
- Operational failures (outages, product defects)
- Financial crises (runway, fraud)
- Legal or regulatory incidents
- **When NOT to use**: Routine issues (use standard processes), or strategic pivots (use change management)


## When NOT to Use

- When the situation requires professional counseling or therapy
- For high-stakes decisions that need expert judgment
- When the skill conflicts with cultural or personal values


## Core Principles

- **Start small** — Begin with manageable crisis management practices and build incrementally
- **Be consistent** — Daily practice beats occasional intense effort
- **Track progress** — Measure improvement to maintain motivation
- **Reflect regularly** — Review what's working and adjust your approach

## Daily Practice

1. **Morning intention** — Set a specific crisis management goal for the day
2. **Active practice** — Apply crisis management techniques during real situations
3. **Evening reflection** — Review the day's crisis management moments and lessons learned
4. **Journal entry** — Record insights, wins, and areas for improvement

## Frameworks

- **OODA Loop** — Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — for rapid crisis management decision cycles
- **PDCA Cycle** — Plan, Do, Check, Act — for iterative crisis management improvement
- **After-Action Review** — What happened? Why? What to improve?
- **Deliberate Practice** — Focused effort on specific crisis management weaknesses

## Common Pitfalls

- **Perfectionism** — Waiting for ideal conditions instead of starting now
- **Inconsistency** — Practicing sporadically instead of building a routine
- **Isolation** — Not seeking feedback or accountability partners
- **Overwhelm** — Trying to improve everything at once instead of focusing

## Resources

- Keep a crisis management journal for tracking progress
- Find an accountability partner or mentor
