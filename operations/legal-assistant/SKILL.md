## Contract Review Checklist

> Run this checklist BEFORE signing ANY contract. Red flags = negotiate or reject.

### Section 1: Parties & Authority

- [ ] Are both parties correctly identified? (Full legal name, address, ID/NPWP)
- [ ] Does the signatory have authority? (Director, CEO, or POA holder)
- [ ] Is the counterparty a real legal entity? (Check AHU/SIPP for PT, personal NIK for individual)
- [ ] Any spelling errors in names/entity names? (Can cause enforcement issues)

### Section 2: Scope of Work

- [ ] Is the deliverable specific and measurable? ("50 TikTok videos" not "content creation")
- [ ] Are revision limits defined? (Max 2 rounds is standard)
- [ ] Are acceptance criteria defined? (Who decides "done"?)
- [ ] Are exclusions listed? (What's NOT included)
- [ ] Does scope creep protection exist? ("Additional work billed separately")

### Section 3: Payment Terms 💰

- [ ] Payment amount clearly stated in IDR (or USD if international)
- [ ] Payment schedule: milestone-based vs. time-based?
- [ ] Late payment penalty defined? (2% per month is standard)
- [ ] Invoice process defined? (Net-30, Net-14?)
- [ ] What triggers final payment? (Delivery + approval, not just delivery)
- [ ] VAT (PPN 11%) treatment — who pays?
- [ ] PPh (income tax) — who is responsible?
- [ ] Expense reimbursement policy defined?

🔴 **RED FLAG:** "Payment upon satisfaction" with no objective criteria — reject this.
🔴 **RED FLAG:** No payment schedule at all — always insist on milestone payments.

### Section 4: Intellectual Property

- [ ] Who owns the output? (Work-for-hire = client, License = creator keeps)
- [ ] Are pre-existing IP assets protected? (Your tools, templates, methods)
- [ ] Are third-party licenses covered? (Music, stock photos, fonts)
- [ ] Moral rights clause? (Indonesia: creators have moral rights even if IP transferred)
- [ ] What happens to IP if contract terminates early?

🔴 **RED FLAG:** "All IP including pre-existing tools" transferred to client — negotiate carve-out.

### Section 5: Confidentiality / NDA

- [ ] NDA duration defined? (2-5 years standard, "forever" is suspicious)
- [ ] What counts as confidential? (Should be defined, not "everything")
- [ ] Exceptions listed? (Public knowledge, required by law, independent development)
- [ ] Permitted disclosures? (Employees on need-to-know basis)
- [ ] Return/destruction of data clause?

### Section 6: Term & Termination

- [ ] Contract start and end dates clear?
- [ ] Auto-renewal clause? (Can be a trap — set calendar reminder)
- [ ] Notice period for termination? (30 days minimum is fair)
- [ ] Termination for cause vs. convenience — both defined?
- [ ] What happens to ongoing work if terminated early?
- [ ] Survival clause — what continues after termination? (NDA, IP, payment obligations)

🔴 **RED FLAG:** No termination clause at all — contract could run forever.
🔴 **RED FLAG:** Auto-renewal with no exit window — negotiate 60-day opt-out before renewal.

### Section 7: Liability & Indemnification

- [ ] Liability cap defined? (Typically 1-2x contract value)
- [ ] Consequential/indirect damages excluded? (Lost profits — you don't want this risk)
- [ ] Indemnification for IP infringement? (If you use 3rd party content)
- [ ] Insurance requirements? (Often overkill for small contracts, but check)
- [ ] Force majeure clause? (COVID, natural disaster, government action)

🔴 **RED FLAG:** Unlimited liability — always negotiate a cap.
🔴 **RED FLAG:** You indemnify THEM for their own negligence — reject.

### Section 8: Dispute Resolution

- [ ] Governing law specified? (Indonesia or another jurisdiction?)
- [ ] Dispute resolution mechanism? (Negotiation → Mediation → Arbitration/Court)
- [ ] Venue specified? (Your local court is better for you)
- [ ] Arbitration binding? (BANI is usually faster than courts)
- [ ] Attorney fees — who pays if you win?

### Section 9: Indonesian Law Specifics

- [ ] Materai clause / physical materai attached?
- [ ] Bahasa Indonesia version? (Courts may require Indonesian language version)
- [ ] Bilingual contract — which version prevails? (Should say "Indonesian prevails")
- [ ] NPWP number correct? (Required for contracts involving payments)
- [ ] PPh withholding tax obligations mentioned?

## IP Protection

Strategies for protecting intellectual property: patents, trade secrets, copyrights.


### Copyright (Hak Cipta) — Indonesia

**Auto-protection:** Copyright in Indonesia attaches automatically upon creation.
No registration required, but registration provides legal proof of ownership.

**Protected works:**
- Written content (scripts, articles, posts)
- Video content (TikTok videos, YouTube)
- Music (originals, covers with license)
- Software code
- Graphic designs
- Logos (also potentially Trademark)

**Duration:**
- Creator's lifetime + 70 years (individual)
- 50 years from publication (corporate/anonymous)

**Registration (Optional but Recommended):**
- Register at DJKI (Direktorat Jenderal Kekayaan Intelektual)
- URL: https://dgip.go.id / https://pdki-indonesia.dgip.go.id
- Cost: Rp 200,000 – 800,000 per work
- Processing time: 1-3 months

**When to Register:**
- High-value content (viral potential)
- Branded characters, mascots
- Original music / jingles
- Proprietary training materials or courses

**Copyright Notice Template:**
```
© 2026 BerkahKarya / {CREATOR_NAME}. Hak Cipta Dilindungi.
Penggunaan tanpa izin tertulis dilarang.
All rights reserved. Unauthorized use prohibited.
```

### Trademark (Merek) — Indonesia

**What to Trademark:**
- Brand name: BerkahKarya
- Product names: JENDRALBOT
- Logo marks
- Slogans (if distinctive enough)

**Classes relevant to BerkahKarya:**
| Nice Class | Covers |
|------------|--------|
| Class 35 | Advertising, influencer marketing, talent management |
| Class 38 | Broadcasting, streaming, social media |
| Class 41 | Entertainment, content creation, education |
| Class 42 | Software, tech services |

**Registration Process:**
1. Trademark search: https://pdki-indonesia.dgip.go.id (check existing)
2. File at DJKI: https://merek.dgip.go.id
3. Cost: ~Rp 1,800,000 per class (online)
4. Examination: 12-18 months
5. Valid: 10 years, renewable

**Priority Rule:** First to file wins in Indonesia (not first to use).
Register early, even before launch.

## GDPR & Privacy Compliance

> BerkahKarya operates digital products. If ANY user is EU-based, GDPR applies.
> Even for Indonesia-only products, UU PDP (Personal Data Protection) now applies.

### Indonesia: UU No. 27/2022 (UU PDP)

Effective: October 2024. Applies to ALL data controllers processing Indonesian personal data.

**Key requirements:**
- [ ] Explicit consent before collecting personal data
- [ ] Purpose limitation (collect only what you need)
- [ ] Data minimization
- [ ] Right to access, correction, deletion
- [ ] Data breach notification within 14 days to KOMINFO
- [ ] Data Protection Officer (DPO) — required for large-scale processing
- [ ] Privacy policy in Bahasa Indonesia

**What counts as personal data:**
- Name, email, phone, address
- NIK (National ID)
- IP address, device ID
- Behavioral data (clicks, purchases)
- Location data

**For small digital products (JENDRALBOT, etc.):**
- [ ] Privacy policy published
- [ ] Cookie consent (if applicable)
- [ ] Email unsubscribe mechanism
- [ ] Data deletion request process (can be email)

---

### GDPR Quick Compliance (EU users)

**Legal basis for processing (pick one per data type):**
1. **Consent** — explicit opt-in (best for marketing emails)
2. **Contract** — necessary to fulfill service
3. **Legitimate interest** — analytics, fraud prevention
4. **Legal obligation** — tax records

**Minimum Requirements:**
- [ ] Privacy policy in plain language
- [ ] Cookie banner with accept/reject
- [ ] Double opt-in for email marketing
- [ ] Unsubscribe in every email
- [ ] Data processing records
- [ ] Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- [ ] No selling data to third parties without consent
- [ ] Data breach response plan

**GDPR vs UU PDP comparison:**

| Aspect | GDPR | UU PDP Indonesia |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| Penalty | Up to 4% global revenue | Up to 2% annual revenue or Rp 2T |
| Consent | Explicit, withdrawable | Similar |
| DPO | Required for large processors | Similar |
| Breach notification | 72 hours to authority | 14 days |
| Extraterritorial | Yes | Yes |

## Indonesian Business Compliance

Indonesian regulatory requirements: OSS, KBLI, tax registration, BPJS.


### PT (Perseroan Terbatas) Requirements

**Minimum for PT:**
- 2+ shareholders (can be yourself + family member)
- 1 Director + 1 Commissioner (can be same people if only 2 shareholders)
- Minimum capital: Rp 50,000,000 (stated), no mandatory deposit requirement for PT Perorangan (new single-owner PT)
- NPWP (company tax ID)
- NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha) via OSS: https://oss.go.id

**PT Perorangan (Single-Owner PT) — New Option:**
- 1 person = shareholder + director
- Capital < Rp 5B (micro/small category)
- Simpler setup, less admin
- Cannot be used for certain licensed activities

**Setup Process:**
```
1. Akta Pendirian by Notaris → ~Rp 3-5 juta
2. AHU (Ministry of Law) approval → online via SABH
3. SK Kemenkumham (legal entity status)
4. NPWP Perusahaan → KPP (Tax Office)
5. NIB via OSS (oss.go.id) → activates KBLI business license
6. PKP (Pengusaha Kena Pajak) registration if revenue > Rp 4.8B/year
```

**Annual Compliance Checklist:**
- [ ] SPT Tahunan Badan (corporate annual tax) — deadline: April 30 each year
- [ ] SPT Masa (monthly tax reports) — due: 20th of following month
- [ ] BPJS Ketenagakerjaan + BPJS Kesehatan enrollment for employees
- [ ] Annual Shareholders Meeting (RUPS) and minutes
- [ ] Financial statements (simple P&L acceptable for small PT)
- [ ] NIB renewal if required by activity
- [ ] LPSE/procurement registration if doing government work

---

### PKP (Pengusaha Kena Pajak — VAT Registration)

**When required:** Annual turnover > Rp 4,800,000,000 (Rp 4.8 billion)
**Optional:** Can register voluntarily if you want to claim input VAT

**Obligations once PKP:**
- Charge PPN 11% on all taxable sales
- Issue Faktur Pajak (tax invoice) for every transaction
- File SPT Masa PPN by 30th of following month
- Reconcile with eSPT/DJP Online

**For digital products sold to Indonesian users:**
Foreign digital companies: PPN 10% collected by marketplace (Google Play, App Store, etc.)
Indonesian companies: Standard PKP rules apply

---

### SPT (Surat Pemberitahuan Pajak)

**Personal (OP):**
- SPT 1770 (business income) — April 30 deadline
- SPT 1770S (employment + other income < Rp 60M)
- SPT 1770SS (employment only < Rp 60M)

**Corporate:**
- SPT 1771 — April 30 (3 months after fiscal year end)
- PPh 21: Monthly payroll tax — filed 20th of following month
- PPh 23: Withholding on services — 2% if vendor has NPWP, 4% without
- PPh 4(2): Final tax on certain transactions (rent, construction, etc.)

**Common Withholding Taxes for BerkahKarya:**
| Transaction | Rate (with NPWP) | Rate (without NPWP) |
|------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Service fee to individual | PPh 21 progressive | PPh 21 progressive |
| Service fee to company | PPh 23: 2% | PPh 23: 4% |
| Talent commission | PPh 21 or 23 | Higher rate |
| Rental | PPh 4(2): 10% (final) | Same |
| Interest | PPh 23: 15% | 30% |

**DJP Online:** https://djponline.pajak.go.id
**e-Faktur:** https://efaktur.pajak.go.id (for PKP)

### Step 3: BANI Arbitration (if needed)

- Website: https://www.bani-arb.org
- Filing fee: Calculated based on dispute value (~1-3% of claim)
- Average resolution: 6-12 months (vs. 2-5 years in court)
- Decision: Binding and enforceable
- Best for: Commercial disputes > Rp 100 juta

**When to escalate to court (Pengadilan Negeri):**
- Arbitration clause not in original contract
- Criminal element involved (fraud, forgery)
- Need urgent injunction (PKPU/kepailitan for bankruptcy)

## DMCA Takedown Process

> Use when your content is stolen and posted on other platforms.

### Step 1: Document the Infringement

```
Screenshot (with timestamp/URL visible):
- Your original content: URL + date created
- Infringing content: URL + date first seen
- Platform: TikTok / YouTube / Instagram / Website

Proof of ownership:
- Your account that originally posted
- File metadata, raw footage, creation date
- Signed contracts if content was commissioned
```

### Step 2: Takedown by Platform

**TikTok:**
- URL: https://www.tiktok.com/legal/report/copyright
- Response time: 1-5 business days
- If no response: repeat + escalate

**YouTube:**
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/copyright_complaint_form
- Use Copyright Match Tool if YouTube Partner
- Response time: 1-3 days
- Counter-notice window: 10 business days (other party can dispute)

**Instagram / Facebook:**
- URL: https://www.facebook.com/help/contact/1758255661104383
- IP portal: https://www.facebook.com/help/contact/634636770043106

**General Website (any host):**
1. Find host via: `whois domain.com` or https://www.who.is
2. Find DMCA contact at: https://www.dmca.com/lookup.aspx
3. Send DMCA notice to hosting provider's abuse@ email

### DMCA Notice Template

```
To: [Platform/Host DMCA Agent]
From: [Your Name / Company]
Date: [Date]

RE: DMCA Copyright Takedown Notice

I am the copyright owner (or authorized agent) of the following original work:

ORIGINAL WORK:
- Description: [e.g., TikTok video titled "..."]
- URL: [Your original post URL]
- Date created/published: [Date]

INFRINGING CONTENT:
- URL: [Infringing content URL]
- Platform: [TikTok/YouTube/etc.]
- Description: [How it infringes]

I have a good faith belief that the use of the material in the manner
complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent,
or the law.

I swear, under penalty of perjury, that the information in this
notification is accurate, and that I am the copyright owner or
authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

Electronic Signature: [Your Name]
Contact: [email@domain.com]
Physical Address: [Your address — required for DMCA]
Date: [Date]
```

### Indonesia-Specific: UU ITE + Hak Cipta

For Indonesian platforms, cite:
- UU No. 28/2014 (Hak Cipta), Pasal 9 & 113
- UU No. 11/2008 (ITE) for online infringement
- Report to KOMINFO if platform is unresponsive: https://aduankonten.id

## Software Licensing Guide

Open source license compatibility, commercial licensing, and SaaS terms.


### Choose Your License

| License | Commercial Use | Modification | Redistribute | Best For |
|---------|---------------|--------------|--------------|----------|
| **MIT** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes (even closed) | Open source tools |
| **Apache 2.0** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes + patent rights | Enterprise-friendly OSS |
| **GPL 3.0** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Must be GPL | Force open source |
| **AGPL 3.0** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Must be AGPL + SaaS | Prevent SaaS loopholes |
| **BSL 1.1** | ❌ Restricted | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes (non-competing) | Business source (Elastic-style) |
| **Proprietary** | ✅ Your terms | ❌ No | ❌ No | Commercial products |
| **CC BY 4.0** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ With attribution | Content, not code |
| **CC BY-NC 4.0** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes | ✅ Non-commercial only | Educational content |

### Decision Tree

```
Is it code?
├── YES
│   ├── Want to keep it closed? → Proprietary License
│   ├── Want community contributions? 
│   │   ├── OK with commercial use? → MIT or Apache 2.0
│   │   └── Want others to open-source too? → GPL 3.0
│   └── It's a SaaS service (cloud use)?
│       └── Want to prevent SaaS competitors? → AGPL 3.0
└── NO (content, designs, courses)
    ├── OK with commercial reuse? → CC BY 4.0
    ├── Non-commercial only? → CC BY-NC 4.0
    └── No reuse allowed? → Copyright (All Rights Reserved)
```

### For BerkahKarya Products

| Product | Recommended License | Reason |
|---------|--------------------|-|
| Internal scripts/tools | Proprietary | Competitive advantage |
| Public utilities/helpers | MIT | Community goodwill |
| JENDRALBOT courses | CC BY-NC 4.0 | Share but prevent resale |
| SaaS platform | Proprietary + ToS | Revenue protection |
| Open-source contributions | MIT or Apache 2.0 | Standard |

## Employment Law

Key aspects of legal-assistant relevant to this section.


### PKWT vs PKWTT — Decision Matrix

| Factor | PKWT (Fixed Term) | PKWTT (Permanent) |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| Duration | Max 2+2 years | No limit |
| Probation | Not allowed | Max 3 months |
| Severance if contract ends | ❌ None (normal end) | ✅ Full UPSEG |
| Severance if terminated early | ✅ Remaining salary | ✅ Full UPSEG |
| Social security (BPJS) | ✅ Required | ✅ Required |
| Annual leave | ✅ Required | ✅ Required |
| Suitable for | Project-based, seasonal | Core team, ongoing roles |
| Regulated by | PP No. 35/2021 | UU No. 13/2003 |

### When to Use PKWT

**ALLOWED (PKWT valid):**
- One-time project with clear endpoint
- Seasonal work (Ramadan campaign, year-end)
- New product launch team
- Replacement for employee on leave
- Trial of new business activity

**NOT ALLOWED (must use PKWTT):**
- Ongoing core business activities
- Regular, continuous work (daily operations)
- Work that won't have a natural end

> 🔴 **Risk:** Using PKWT for ongoing work = courts may rule it as PKWTT
> = retroactive severance obligation. Don't be cheap. Use PKWTT for core team.

---

### PKWT Requirements (PP No. 35/2021)

- [ ] Written contract (wajib tertulis, tidak boleh lisan)
- [ ] Registered at Disnaker within 3 days of signing
- [ ] In Indonesian language
- [ ] States: type of work, start/end date, work location, salary
- [ ] PKWT compensation (uang kompensasi) must be paid at contract end:
  - 1 month salary per year worked (proportional for < 12 months)
  - Paid on the last day of contract

**PKWT Compensation Formula:**
```
If worked ≥ 12 months: 1 month salary × years worked
If worked < 12 months: (months worked / 12) × 1 month salary

Example: 6 months PKWT at Rp 3,000,000/month
Compensation = (6/12) × 3,000,000 = Rp 1,500,000
```

---

### PKWTT Severance (UPSEG Formula)

**U** = Uang Pesangon (Severance Pay)
**S** = Uang Penghargaan Masa Kerja (Long Service Award)
**E** = Uang Penggantian Hak (Rights Replacement)
**G** = Ganti Rugi (Damages — if applicable)

**Uang Pesangon (U):**
```
< 1 year    = 1 month salary
1-2 years   = 2 months salary
2-3 years   = 3 months salary
3-4 years   = 4 months salary
4-5 years   = 5 months salary
5-6 years   = 6 months salary
6-7 years   = 7 months salary
7-8 years   = 8 months salary
≥ 8 years   = 9 months salary
```

**Uang Penghargaan (S):**
```
3-6 years    = 2 months salary
6-9 years    = 3 months salary
9-12 years   = 4 months salary
12-15 years  = 5 months salary
15-18 years  = 6 months salary
18-21 years  = 7 months salary
21-24 years  = 8 months salary
≥ 24 years   = 10 months salary
```

**For standard termination (JKK reason):**
Full UPSEG = U × 1.0 + S × 1.0 + E
JKP program (if BPJS enrolled): government pays 45% of 6 months' wages

### Freelancer vs. Employee — Legal Test

Indonesian courts look at these factors (Subordination Test):

| Factor | Freelancer (PKS) | Employee (PKWT/PKWTT) |
|--------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Work hours | Flexible | Set by company |
| Work location | Anywhere | Company premises/policy |
| Tools | Their own | Company provided |
| Supervision | Minimal, output-based | Direct supervision |
| Exclusivity | Often works for multiple | Usually exclusive |
| Tax treatment | PPh 21 final or PPh 23 | PPh 21 gross-up |
| BPJS | Not required | Required |

> 🔴 **Risk:** If a "freelancer" looks like an employee (set hours, close supervision, exclusive), courts may reclassify → retroactive BPJS + severance liability.

## Red Flag Reference Card

Quick-scan list for any contract review:

```
🔴 REJECT/RENEGOTIATE IMMEDIATELY:
  □ "Payment upon our satisfaction" — no objective criteria
  □ Unlimited liability on your side
  □ All IP transferred including pre-existing tools
  □ Non-compete > 12 months (unenforceable in Indonesia)
  □ Auto-renewal with no opt-out window
  □ No termination clause
  □ Governing law = foreign country (disadvantages you)
  □ You indemnify them for THEIR negligence
  □ No payment schedule (open-ended obligation)
  □ Confidentiality that never expires ("forever")

⚠️ NEGOTIATE BEFORE SIGNING:
  □ Notice period < 14 days (too short)
  □ Liability cap < 1x contract value
  □ Revision limit not defined
  □ No force majeure clause
  □ Late payment penalty missing
  □ No dispute escalation path
  □ Ambiguous deliverable definition
  □ "Reasonable" used too often without definition

✅ GOOD SIGNS — Contract is fair:
  □ Liability capped at 1-2x contract value
  □ Clear deliverables with acceptance criteria
  □ Mutual termination rights with notice
  □ Governing law = Indonesia
  □ Both parties have exit options
  □ Payment on milestone, not just "on completion"
  □ IP carve-outs for pre-existing work
  □ NDA duration = 2-5 years (reasonable)
```
