## Compliance by Region

### Core Regulations to Know

| Region | Regulation | Key Requirements |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| EU/EEA | GDPR | Consent before data collection, right to erasure, DPO required at scale |
| Indonesia | UU PDP (2022) | Similar to GDPR, data localization for certain sectors, fines up to 2% revenue |
| USA | CAN-SPAM | Unsubscribe mechanism, physical address, no deceptive subjects |
| USA (California) | CCPA/CPRA | Opt-out of data sale, data deletion rights, privacy notices |
| Global | Platform ToS | Each ad/social platform has its own ad policies that override local law |

### Universal Marketing Compliance Rules
- Never buy email lists. Always use opt-in
- Include unsubscribe link in every commercial email
- Include business physical address in email footers
- State clearly who is sending the communication
- Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days (CAN-SPAM) or immediately (GDPR)
- Retain records of consent (when, where, what they agreed to)
- Disclose sponsored content and affiliate relationships
- Don't make unsubstantiated claims (health, income, guaranteed results)

### Cookie & Tracking Consent
- EU: Explicit consent before any non-essential cookies/tracking
- Indonesia: Consent required under PDP law for personal data processing
- Best practice globally: Cookie consent banner with granular options
- Always document what you track and why (privacy policy)

## Indonesia & UMKM Marketing

### Indonesian Digital Marketing Landscape
- 212M+ internet users (2026), mobile-first market
- WhatsApp is THE dominant messaging platform (~90%+ penetration)
- Instagram, TikTok, Facebook most-used social platforms
- Shopee, Tokopedia (now TikTok Shop), Lazada dominate e-commerce
- Bahasa Indonesia is mandatory for consumer-facing content
- Cash on Delivery (COD) still significant for e-commerce
- Trust is built through personal relationships and community endorsement

### UMKM-Specific Marketing Strategy
Indonesian SMBs (UMKM) have unique constraints and opportunities:

**Start simple, scale gradually:**
```
Tahap 1 (Month 1-2): WhatsApp Business + Instagram
  - Set up WhatsApp Business profile with catalog
  - Post consistently on Instagram (3-5x/week)
  - Respond to all inquiries within 1 hour

Tahap 2 (Month 3-4): Add content + basic SEO
  - Create Google Business Profile
  - Start a simple website or landing page
  - Collect customer testimonials systematically

Tahap 3 (Month 5-6): Automation + paid ads
  - Set up WhatsApp auto-replies
  - Test small-budget Meta Ads (Rp 50,000-100,000/day)
  - Build email list from WhatsApp contacts (with consent)

Tahap 4 (Month 7+): Optimize + scale
  - Analyze which channels drive actual revenue
  - Double down on top performers
  - Build referral system
```

**Pricing for IDR Market:**
- Charm pricing works differently: Rp 99.000 vs Rp 100.000
- "Mulai dari Rp X" (starting from) for range pricing
- Bundling is very effective in Indonesian market
- Payment installments (cicilan) increase conversion significantly
- Free shipping (gratis ongkir) is a major conversion driver

**Content Language Rules:**
- All customer-facing content in Bahasa Indonesia
- Mix formal (for professional/B2B) and informal (for consumer/social)
- Local dialects can work for hyper-local targeting
- English OK for tech-savvy/premium segments as accent language
- Always proofread — bad Indonesian is worse than no Indonesian

### Indonesian PDP Law Requirements
- **Consent:** Explicit consent required before processing personal data
- **Purpose limitation:** Data only used for stated purposes
- **Data subject rights:** Access, correction, deletion, portability
- **Breach notification:** Within 3×24 hours
- **Cross-border transfer:** Adequate protection required in receiving country
- **Penalties:** Administrative fines up to 2% of annual revenue; criminal penalties possible
- **Data Protection Officer:** Required for large-scale processing

## WhatsApp Marketing

### WhatsApp Business Features for Marketing

| Feature | Use Case |
|---------|----------|
| **Business Profile** | Store hours, address, website, catalog |
| **Catalog** | Product gallery with prices, descriptions |
| **Quick Replies** | Saved responses for FAQs |
| **Auto-messages** | Greeting, away, and auto-reply messages |
| **Labels** | Organize contacts (New Customer, VIP, Follow-up) |
| **Broadcast Lists** | Send to multiple contacts (max 256 per list) |
| **Status Updates** | 24-hour stories for promotions, behind-the-scenes |
| **Payment Links** | Share payment links directly in chat |

### WhatsApp Marketing Best Practices

**Do:**
- Get explicit opt-in before adding to broadcast lists
- Respond to messages within 1 hour during business hours
- Use rich media (images, videos, PDFs) in messages
- Personalize messages with customer name and purchase history
- Use Labels to segment customers for targeted broadcasts
- Post Status updates daily for promotions and social proof
- Create template responses for common queries

**Don't:**
- Spam contacts or add people without consent
- Send more than 1-2 broadcast messages per week
- Use automation that feels robotic — keep it human
- Share customer data or conversations without permission
- Send messages outside reasonable hours (before 8am or after 9pm)

## Localization Strategy

### Localization vs Translation
Translation = changing words. Localization = adapting the entire experience.

**What to localize:**
- Language and tone (formal vs informal varies by culture)
- Currency and number formatting
- Date/time format (DD/MM/YYYY in most of Asia/Europe)
- Payment methods (local payment gateways, COD, installments)
- Social proof (local testimonials, local company logos)
- Images (people, settings, and aesthetics that reflect local culture)
- Cultural references and humor (what's funny in one market is confusing in another)
- Channel selection (WhatsApp in Indonesia, LINE in Japan, WeChat in China)
- CTAs and urgency norms (aggressive urgency feels different across cultures)

### Multi-Language Content Strategy
1. **Primary language first:** Build content engine in primary market language
2. **Translate core pages:** Homepage, pricing, key landing pages
3. **Localize high-traffic content:** Blog posts driving organic traffic
4. **Local social presence:** Separate or mixed accounts depending on audience size
5. **Hreflang tags:** Proper SEO markup for multi-language sites

## Ad Platform Compliance

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Ad Policies
- No misleading claims or "before/after" that imply guaranteed results
- No content that asserts or implies personal attributes (race, religion, health)
- Financial products/services require disclaimers
- Alcohol, gambling, supplements have restricted targeting
- Landing page must match ad content (no bait-and-switch)
- Political/social issue ads require identity verification

### Google Ads Policies
- No misleading content, deceptive URLs, or false claims
- Healthcare and pharmaceutical ads require certification
- Financial services ads must comply with local regulations
- Trademark rules for competitor keywords vary by country
- Legal disclaimers required for specific industries
- Destination page must be functional and relevant

### TikTok Ad Policies
- No extreme claims or "guaranteed results"
- Products must be available for purchase in target country
- Age-gate requirements for certain product categories
- Influencer content must be disclosed as sponsored

## Content Compliance Checklist

Before publishing any marketing content, verify:

- [ ] **Claims are substantiated** — no unverifiable statistics or results guarantees
- [ ] **Testimonials are real** — from actual customers with their consent
- [ ] **Disclaimers present** where required (financial, health, legal)
- [ ] **Affiliate/sponsored content disclosed** clearly
- [ ] **No competitor disparagement** — compare features, don't attack brands
- [ ] **Privacy compliant** — opt-in, unsubscribe, data usage disclosed
- [ ] **Platform ToS compliant** — ad content meets platform-specific rules
- [ ] **Accessibility** — alt text on images, captions on video, contrast ratios
- [ ] **Cultural sensitivity** — no content that could offend target audience
- [ ] **Local law compliant** — PDP (Indonesia), GDPR (EU), CAN-SPAM (US)
- [ ] **No copyrighted material** — images licensed, fonts licensed, music licensed
- [ ] **AI-generated content reviewed** — fact-checked, brand voice verified, not robotic
