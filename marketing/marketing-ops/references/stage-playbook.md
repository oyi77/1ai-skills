# Stage-Based Marketing Playbook

Stop doing everything. Do the RIGHT thing for YOUR stage.
60% of startups fail from premature scaling — doing $100K tactics at $0 MRR.

---

## Stage Assessment

### What Stage Am I In?

| Stage | MRR | Users | Focus | Marketing Time |
|-------|-----|-------|-------|---------------|
| **0: Validate** | $0 | 0-10 | Does anyone want this? | 20% (80% product) |
| **1: Foundation** | $0-1K | 10-50 | Find first paying customers | 30% |
| **2: Traction** | $1K-10K | 50-500 | Find repeatable channel | 40% |
| **3: Growth** | $10K-50K | 500-5K | Scale winning channels | 40% |
| **4: Scale** | $50K-100K+ | 5K+ | Multi-channel, optimize | 30% (hire) |

## Stage 0: Validate ($0 MRR)

**Goal:** Prove someone will pay for this.

**DO:**
- Talk to 20 potential customers (interviews, not surveys)
- Build a landing page that explains the value prop
- Collect emails from interested people (waitlist)
- Pre-sell or offer founding member discounts
- Post in 3-5 communities where your audience lives

**DON'T:**
- Run paid ads
- Build SEO content
- Create social media accounts
- Design a logo or brand kit
- Build features nobody asked for

**Weekly time:** 5 hours marketing / 20 hours product
**Budget:** $0
**Key metric:** Conversations had + waitlist signups

---

## Stage 1: Foundation ($0-1K MRR)

**Goal:** Get first 10-50 paying customers. Find product-market fit signals.

**DO:**
- Set up marketing-profile.yml (brand identity, ICP, voice)
- Create your website/landing page with clear value prop
- Write 3-5 foundational blog posts targeting long-tail keywords
- Set up email capture + simple welcome sequence (3 emails)
- Manual outreach: 10-20 personalized emails/DMs per day
- Post on Product Hunt, relevant directories, communities
- Set up Google Analytics + basic tracking
- Collect every piece of feedback and testimonial

**DON'T:**
- Hire a marketer or agency
- Spend more than $200/month on ads
- Try to be on every social platform
- Build complex automation
- Obsess over branding/design

**Weekly time:** 8 hours marketing / 15 hours product
**Budget:** $0-200/month
**Key metric:** New paying customers per week
**Channel-model fit:** Low ARPU (<$50/mo) → organic, viral, community.
High ARPU (>$200/mo) → outbound, demos, partnerships.

## Stage 2: Traction ($1K-10K MRR)

**Goal:** Find the ONE channel that works and double down.

**DO:**
- Analyze which channel brought your first customers → DOUBLE DOWN
- Start SEO seriously: 2 blog posts/week, keyword-targeted
- Build email list to 500+ subscribers with lead magnets
- Set up proper CRM (HubSpot free, Twenty, or Pipedrive)
- Test small-budget paid ads ($500-1K/month on 1-2 platforms)
- Create a referral mechanism (even if simple)
- Start collecting and displaying social proof systematically
- Set up basic marketing automation (welcome, nurture, follow-up)

**DON'T:**
- Spread budget across 5+ channels (focus on 1-2)
- Hire full-time marketer yet
- Rebrand or redesign everything
- Build complex funnels before simple ones work
- Ignore the channel that's already working

**Weekly time:** 10 hours marketing / 15 hours product
**Budget:** $500-2,000/month
**Key metric:** Monthly growth rate (target: 15-20% MoM)

### The Channel Identification Test
Run each potential channel for 2-4 weeks with minimal budget.
Measure cost per acquisition. Kill channels where CPA > LTV/3.
Scale channels where CPA < LTV/5.

```
Test 3-5 channels simultaneously:
  □ SEO/Content (free, slow, compounds)
  □ Paid Meta Ads (fast feedback, costs money)
  □ Cold outreach (free, manual, high conversion)
  □ Community/Reddit (free, slow, authentic)
  □ Partnerships (free, relationship-dependent)

After 4 weeks, rank by: CPA, volume potential, effort required
Pick TOP 2 → invest 80% of marketing time/budget there
```

## Stage 3: Growth ($10K-50K MRR)

**Goal:** Scale winning channels. Add second and third channel.

**DO:**
- Scale primary channel aggressively (if CPA still healthy)
- Add 1-2 secondary channels based on Stage 2 testing
- Invest in GEO/AEO (AI search optimization)
- Build programmatic SEO if keyword patterns exist
- Launch formal affiliate/referral program
- Create case studies from best customers
- Consider first marketing hire or fractional CMO
- Set up proper attribution and reporting
- Build marketing automation for full lifecycle

**DON'T:**
- Change what's working (optimize, don't replace)
- Hire senior before proving you need the role
- Over-invest in brand marketing (still ROI-focused)
- Ignore churn (retention marketing starts here)

**Weekly time:** 12 hours marketing / 12 hours product
**Budget:** $2K-10K/month
**Key metric:** LTV:CAC ratio (target: 3:1+) and payback period (<12 months)

---

## Stage 4: Scale ($50K-100K+ MRR)

**Goal:** Multi-channel growth machine. Optimize unit economics.

**DO:**
- Hire first full-time marketer (T-shaped generalist)
- Build multi-channel presence (3-5 channels)
- Invest in brand marketing (thought leadership, PR)
- Advanced analytics and attribution
- International expansion if applicable
- Strategic partnerships and integrations
- RevOps: align marketing, sales, customer success data

**Budget:** $10K-50K/month (8-15% of ARR)
**Key metric:** Net Revenue Retention (target: >110%)

## Budget Allocation by Stage

```
Stage 1 ($0-1K MRR):   100% organic + sweat equity
Stage 2 ($1K-10K):     60% content/SEO, 30% paid testing, 10% tools
Stage 3 ($10K-50K):    40% paid acquisition, 30% content/SEO, 20% retention, 10% testing
Stage 4 ($50K-100K+):  35% paid, 25% content, 20% retention, 10% brand, 10% testing
```

## When to Hire Your First Marketer

**Signals you need a marketing hire:**
- You're spending 15+ hours/week on marketing AND it's working
- You have a proven, repeatable channel that just needs more volume
- Your MRR is $20K+ and growth is limited by marketing capacity
- You have clear marketing SOPs that someone else can follow

**What to hire:**
- First hire: T-shaped generalist (broad skills + deep in your top channel)
- NOT: VP Marketing, CMO, or specialist (too narrow, too expensive)
- Consider: Fractional CMO (5-10 hours/week) before full-time hire
- Consider: Freelancers for specific tasks (content writers, ad managers)

**What to outsource vs keep in-house:**
| Outsource | Keep In-House |
|-----------|--------------|
| Content writing | Strategy & positioning |
| Ad management (once proven) | Customer conversations |
| Design / video production | Brand voice decisions |
| Technical SEO | Pricing & offer design |
| Data entry / CRM cleanup | Performance review & decisions |
