# Ad Copy Optimization Reference

Systematic frameworks for writing, testing, and improving ad copy across platforms.
This isn't about writing "good" ads — it's about building a testing system that
finds winning copy predictably.

---

## The Ad Copy Testing System

### Why Most Ad Copy Fails
- Written by "gut feel" instead of tested frameworks
- Only 1 variant created (no A/B testing)
- No systematic way to identify WHY one ad beats another
- Copy written for the marketer, not the customer
- Same copy used across all platforms and audiences

### The 3×3×3 Testing Framework
For every campaign, create:
- **3 angles** (different value props or pain points)
- **3 hooks** per angle (different opening approaches)
- **3 CTA variants** per winning hook

This gives you 9 initial ads → test → find winner → iterate.

## Ad Copy Anatomy

### Universal Ad Copy Structure
```
[HOOK]       — Stop the scroll (first 1-2 lines)
[PROBLEM]    — Name the pain (1-2 lines)
[SOLUTION]   — Present the answer (2-3 lines)
[PROOF]      — Data, testimonials, social proof (1-2 lines)
[CTA]        — Clear next step (1 line)
```

### The Hook Library (First Line Formulas)

**Question hooks:**
- "Still doing {inefficient thing} manually?"
- "What if you could {dream outcome} in {timeframe}?"
- "Why are {audience} switching from {old way} to {new way}?"

**Stat hooks:**
- "{X}% of {audience} waste {resource} on {problem}."
- "Companies using {method} see {X}% more {result}."
- "We analyzed {number} {things}. Here's what we found."

**Story hooks:**
- "Last {time period}, {customer} was struggling with {problem}..."
- "I used to spend {hours/money} on {task}. Then I discovered..."
- "When {customer name} first tried {product}, they expected {X}. They got {Y}."

**Contrarian hooks:**
- "Everything you've been told about {topic} is wrong."
- "{Common advice} is actually hurting your {metric}."
- "Stop {popular tactic}. Do this instead."

**Direct hooks:**
- "{Result} in {timeframe}. No {common friction}."
- "The {category} tool built for people who hate {category} tools."
- "{Benefit 1}. {Benefit 2}. {Benefit 3}. One platform."

## Platform-Specific Ad Copy Optimization

### Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram)

**Primary Text Optimization:**
- First 125 characters are visible before "See more" — front-load value
- Emojis can increase CTR but test (✅ checkmarks and 🎯 targets work well)
- Line breaks improve readability
- Social proof in opening line lifts CTR: "Join 50,000+ marketers who..."

**Headline Optimization:**
- 40 characters visible — be concise
- Include the primary benefit or offer
- Match the CTA button (if CTA = "Shop Now", headline = benefit of shopping)

**Testing priority:**
1. Primary text (biggest impact on CTR)
2. Creative (image/video paired with copy)
3. Headline
4. Description
5. CTA button

**Winning patterns:**
- UGC-style copy (first person, conversational) outperforms corporate copy
- Specific numbers beat vague claims ("saved 12 hours" > "saves time")
- Addressing objections directly increases conversion ("Yes, it works on mobile too")

### Google Search Ads

**Responsive Search Ads (RSA) Optimization:**
- Write 15 headlines (max) — mix of keyword, benefit, CTA, social proof
- Pin important headlines to Position 1 or 2
- Include primary keyword in at least 3 headlines
- Write 4 descriptions — mix of value prop, features, social proof, CTA
- Use all available ad extensions (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets)

**Headline testing approach:**
```
Category 1: Keyword-focused (3-4 headlines)
  "AI Marketing Automation Tool"
  "Best Marketing Automation Platform"

Category 2: Benefit-focused (3-4 headlines)
  "Save 10 Hours Per Week on Marketing"
  "Increase Revenue 34% With AI Marketing"

Category 3: Social proof (2-3 headlines)
  "Trusted by 5,000+ Marketing Teams"
  "Rated #1 Marketing Platform on G2"

Category 4: CTA/Offer (2-3 headlines)
  "Start Free — No Credit Card"
  "Get a Free Marketing Audit Today"

Category 5: Urgency/Differentiation (2 headlines)
  "The Only Platform That Does X"
  "Limited: First Month Free"
```

### TikTok/Reels Video Ad Copy

**Text overlay optimization (the "copy" of video ads):**
- Hook text: 3-5 words max, bold, readable on mobile
- Each scene needs a text overlay reinforcing the message
- Final frame: CTA text + brand

**Voiceover script optimization:**
- First line = pattern interrupt ("Wait — are you still doing X?")
- Speak to ONE person, not an audience
- Conversational tone (not "announcer voice")
- Under 60 seconds for most products
- End with specific CTA ("Link in bio" / "Click below")

### LinkedIn Ads

**Sponsored Content copy:**
- Professional but human — no corporate jargon
- Lead with insight or data, not product pitch
- Longer primary text performs well (150-300 words)
- Questions in copy drive comments
- Include thought leadership angle

**InMail/Message Ads:**
- Subject line: personalized, specific, under 60 chars
- Body: short (under 500 chars), value-first, single CTA
- Personalization tokens: {{first_name}}, {{company_name}}, {{job_title}}

## Ad Copy Scoring System

### Score each ad variant on these 5 dimensions (1-10 each):

| Dimension | What to Evaluate | Weight |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| **Hook Strength** | Would target audience stop scrolling? | 25% |
| **Clarity** | Is the value prop obvious in 3 seconds? | 25% |
| **Proof** | Is there evidence (data, testimonial, authority)? | 20% |
| **CTA Quality** | Is the next step clear and low-friction? | 15% |
| **Platform Fit** | Does it match platform norms and specs? | 15% |

**Total Score = Weighted average × 10**
- 80-100: Strong — launch and test
- 60-79: Decent — needs refinement
- Below 60: Rewrite from scratch

### Ad Copy Quality Checklist
- [ ] Hook stops the scroll in first 1-2 lines
- [ ] Speaks to target audience specifically (not "everyone")
- [ ] Benefit is clear (not just features)
- [ ] Social proof present (numbers, names, testimonials)
- [ ] Objections addressed (friction reducers near CTA)
- [ ] One clear CTA (not multiple competing asks)
- [ ] Platform character limits respected
- [ ] No jargon the audience wouldn't use
- [ ] A/B test variant ready (minimum 2 versions)
- [ ] Landing page matches ad promise (no bait-and-switch)

## Ad Creative × Copy Pairing

### The Ad System = Creative + Copy Working Together

| Creative Type | Best Copy Pairing |
|--------------|-------------------|
| UGC video | First-person testimonial copy |
| Product demo | Feature → Benefit copy |
| Before/After | Transformation story (BAB) |
| Static image | Bold headline + subheadline |
| Carousel | One benefit per card, sequential story |
| Meme/Trend | Minimal copy, let creative do the work |

### Creative-Copy Mismatch (Common Mistake)
- ❌ Polished brand video + casual "hey, you gotta try this" copy
- ❌ UGC-style creative + corporate "leverage our solution" copy
- ✅ Match the energy: UGC + conversational, Professional + authoritative

## Iteration & Scaling Framework

### Ad Lifecycle
```
Week 1: Launch 9 variants (3 angles × 3 hooks)
Week 2: Kill bottom 6, scale top 3
Week 3: Create 3 new variants riffing on winners
Week 4: Kill underperformers, introduce new angles
Ongoing: Refresh creative every 2-4 weeks (creative fatigue)
```

### Creative Fatigue Signals
- CTR declining 20%+ over 7 days
- Frequency > 3 in 7 days
- CPA rising while impressions stay flat
- Comment sentiment turning negative ("I keep seeing this ad")

### Winning Ad Anatomy (What to Replicate)
When you find a winner, analyze WHY it works:
1. What hook did it use? (replicate the hook type)
2. What proof point resonated? (use in other ads)
3. What emotion did it trigger? (apply to other products)
4. What format was it? (double down on that format)
5. What audience responded? (expand or narrow)
