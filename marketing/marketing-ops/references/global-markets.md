## Market Entry Checklist

Before entering any new market:

### Research Phase
- [ ] Market size and digital penetration rate
- [ ] Dominant social platforms and search engines (not always Google)
- [ ] Local messaging app (often more important than email)
- [ ] Payment methods and preferences (credit card penetration varies wildly)
- [ ] Data privacy regulations (GDPR, PDP, LGPD, PIPA, etc.)
- [ ] Cultural norms around marketing (aggressiveness, humor, formality)
- [ ] Local competitors and their marketing approach
- [ ] Language and dialect considerations
- [ ] Average customer acquisition cost in this market
- [ ] Local influencer landscape

### Execution Phase
- [ ] Translate AND localize core pages (not just Google Translate)
- [ ] Set up local payment gateway
- [ ] Create social presence on locally dominant platforms
- [ ] Build local social proof (testimonials, case studies, reviews)
- [ ] Implement hreflang tags for multi-language SEO
- [ ] Set up local customer support channel (WhatsApp, LINE, etc.)
- [ ] Comply with local data privacy regulations
- [ ] Adapt pricing to local purchasing power
- [ ] Create locally relevant content (not just translated global content)
- [ ] Partner with local influencers or distributors

## Regional Marketing Playbooks

### Southeast Asia (Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines)
- **Mobile-first**: 90%+ access internet via mobile
- **Chat commerce**: Transactions happen inside messaging apps
- **Social commerce**: TikTok Shop, Shopee Live, Instagram Shopping
- **Price sensitivity**: Installment payments, free shipping critical
- **Trust**: Personal recommendations, influencer endorsements
- **Content**: Short-form video dominates (TikTok, Reels)
- **Platforms**: Facebook still strong for older demographics

### Middle East (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar)
- **Visual-first**: Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok dominate
- **Arabic content**: Must support RTL (right-to-left) text
- **Cultural sensitivity**: Respect religious observances (Ramadan = peak marketing season)
- **Premium positioning**: Luxury and quality messaging resonates
- **Influencer-heavy**: KOL marketing is primary discovery channel
- **Payment**: Buy Now Pay Later (Tabby, Tamara) rapidly growing

### Latin America (Brazil, Mexico, Argentina)
- **WhatsApp is everything**: Business communication, payments, customer service
- **Installments**: "Parcelas" (installment payments) are default expectation
- **Price in local currency**: Always show BRL, MXN, ARS
- **Community-driven**: Strong word-of-mouth and family recommendation culture
- **Video content**: YouTube and TikTok primary discovery
- **Marketplace-first**: MercadoLibre/Shopee before your own e-commerce

### Europe (Germany, UK, France, Nordics)
- **Privacy-first**: GDPR compliance mandatory, cookie consent strict
- **Quality over hype**: Factual, evidence-based marketing preferred
- **Sustainability matters**: ESG messaging resonates (especially Nordics)
- **LinkedIn strong for B2B**: Primary professional platform
- **Local trust signals**: Local reviews, local phone numbers, EU hosting
- **Payment diversity**: Each country has preferred methods (Klarna, iDEAL, etc.)

### East Asia (Japan, South Korea)
- **Platform-specific**: Naver (KR), Yahoo Japan (JP), LINE (both)
- **Design standards**: Clean, detailed, information-dense designs preferred
- **Quality expectations**: Very high — no tolerance for poor translations
- **Seasonal marketing**: Cultural events drive major spending peaks
- **Community forums**: Active review and discussion culture
- **Localization depth**: Surface-level translation = brand damage

## Multi-Language SEO (International SEO)

### Hreflang Implementation
```html
<!-- On every localized page -->
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en" href="https://example.com/page" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="id" href="https://example.com/id/page" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="ja" href="https://example.com/ja/page" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://example.com/page" />
```

### URL Structure Options
| Approach | Example | Pros | Cons |
|----------|---------|------|------|
| Subdirectory | example.com/id/ | Easy, shares domain authority | Less local signal |
| Subdomain | id.example.com | Separate management | Splits domain authority |
| ccTLD | example.co.id | Strongest local signal | Expensive, separate domains |

**Recommendation:** Subdirectory for most businesses. ccTLD only if you have
dedicated local presence and resources per market.

### Local Search Engines

| Market | Primary Search Engine | Market Share |

Optimize for local search engines when entering these markets.

## Currency & Pricing Localization

### Pricing Psychology by Market
- **USA/UK**: $X.99 charm pricing works (e.g., $49.99)
- **Europe**: €X,90 format (comma as decimal)
- **Indonesia**: Rp X9.000 (e.g., Rp 99.000, Rp 249.000)
- **Japan**: ¥X,X00 — round numbers preferred (¥5,000 not ¥4,999)
- **India**: ₹X,X99 — charm pricing effective
- **Brazil**: R$ X,90 — installment pricing expected ("12x R$ 49,90")

### Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) Pricing
Consider adjusting pricing based on local purchasing power:
- Charge the same USD price worldwide → maximizes revenue from wealthy markets
- Adjust for PPP → maximizes market penetration in emerging markets
- Hybrid: core product at PPP, premium features at global pricing

Tools for PPP pricing: purchasing-power-parity.com, The Big Mac Index

## Global Compliance Quick Reference

| Regulation | Region | Key Requirement | Penalty |
|-----------|--------|----------------|---------|
| GDPR | EU/EEA | Consent, DPO, right to erasure | Up to 4% of global revenue |
| UU PDP | Indonesia | Consent, data localization for sectors | Up to 2% of annual revenue |
| LGPD | Brazil | Similar to GDPR, DPO required | Up to 2% of revenue |
| PIPA | South Korea | Consent, notification, cross-border rules | Criminal penalties |
| PIPL | China | Consent, data localization mandatory | Up to ¥50M (~$7M) |
| CCPA/CPRA | California, USA | Opt-out, data deletion rights | $7,500 per intentional violation |
| PDPA | Thailand | Consent, DPO, data subject rights | Up to ฿5M (~$140K) |
| APPI | Japan | Consent, cross-border transfer rules | Criminal penalties |

### Universal Best Practices
1. Always get explicit consent before collecting personal data
2. Provide clear privacy policy in local language
3. Implement data deletion capability
4. Log consent records (when, what, how)
5. Appoint a data protection contact for each market
6. Review local ad platform policies per market
