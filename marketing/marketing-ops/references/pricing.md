# Pricing Strategy Reference

Pricing is the single highest-leverage marketing decision. A 1% improvement in
pricing yields 11% more profit (McKinsey). Yet most founders set pricing once
and never revisit it.

---

## Pricing Models

### Model Selection Guide

| Model | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|-------|----------|------|------|
| **Flat rate** | Simple products | Easy to understand | Limits revenue per customer |
| **Tiered** | SaaS, services | Natural upgrade path | Can confuse if too many tiers |
| **Usage-based** | APIs, infrastructure | Fair, scales with value | Revenue unpredictable |
| **Per-seat** | Team tools | Scales with company growth | Penalizes adoption |
| **Freemium** | High-volume products | Low friction acquisition | <5% typically convert |
| **Free trial** | Mid-high price products | Builds habit + endowment | Need great onboarding |
| **One-time** | Digital products, templates | Simple, no churn | No recurring revenue |
| **Credits/tokens** | AI products, variable usage | Flexible, fair | Harder to predict revenue |

### For Solo Founders: Start Simple
- **Under $50/mo:** Freemium + paid tier (or free trial)
- **$50-200/mo:** Free trial (14 days) + self-serve purchase
- **$200+/mo:** Demo-based sales + custom pricing
- **Digital product:** One-time purchase or subscription bundle

## The 3-Tier Pricing Framework

### Structure (Works for Most Businesses)

```
┌───────────────┐  ┌───────────────────┐  ┌──────────────────┐
│   STARTER     │  │    PRO ⭐          │  │   BUSINESS       │
│               │  │  "Most Popular"    │  │                  │
│   ${low}      │  │   ${target}        │  │   ${anchor}      │
│               │  │                    │  │                  │
│  Basic value  │  │  Full value        │  │  Maximum value   │
│  Limited      │  │  Generous limits   │  │  Unlimited/VIP   │
│               │  │                    │  │                  │
│  For: trying  │  │  For: growing      │  │  For: serious    │
└───────────────┘  └───────────────────┘  └──────────────────┘
       10%                  70%                    20%
   of customers         of customers           of customers
```

### Tier Design Rules
1. **Starter** exists to make Pro look valuable (not to be your main revenue)
2. **Pro** is your target — most customers, most revenue
3. **Business** is the anchor — makes Pro feel affordable
4. **Feature distribution:** Give Pro everything most users need. Business gets
   priority support, advanced features, more capacity.
5. **Price ratio:** Roughly 1x : 2.5-3x : 5-8x (e.g., $29 : $79 : $199)

## Value-Based Pricing

### How to Set Prices Based on Value (Not Cost)

```
Step 1: What's the customer's current cost?
  - How much time do they spend on this problem? → Time × hourly rate
  - What tools do they currently pay for? → Direct comparison
  - What's the cost of the problem NOT being solved? → Pain cost

Step 2: What result do your customers get?
  - Time saved: X hours/week × $Y/hour = $Z value/month
  - Revenue gained: X% increase in conversions × revenue = $Z
  - Cost avoided: Replaces {tool/person} costing $Z/month

Step 3: Price at 10-20% of the value delivered
  - If you save them $1,000/month → charge $100-200/month
  - If you help them make $5,000 more → charge $500-1,000/month
  - This gives them 5-10x ROI, making it an easy decision

Step 4: Validate with real conversations
  - "If this saved you 10 hours/week, what would that be worth?"
  - "What are you currently spending to solve this?"
  - Watch for flinching vs nodding at your price
```

## Pricing for Indonesia (IDR Market)

### IDR Pricing Conventions
- Round to nearest Rp 1,000 or Rp 5,000
- Charm pricing works: Rp 99.000 vs Rp 100.000
- Show monthly price, offer annual with discount
- "Mulai dari" (starting from) for range pricing
- Always show crossed-out "before" price for discounts

### Installment Strategy (Critical for Indonesian Market)
- Installments (cicilan) dramatically increase conversion
- Partner with: Kredivo, Akulaku, Home Credit, Atome
- Display as: "Rp 49.900/bulan × 12 bulan" not "Rp 598.800"
- Even digital products benefit from installment options

### Price Anchoring (IDR)
```
Don't: "Langganan Rp 150.000/bulan"
Do:    "Biasanya Rp 300.000/bulan → Spesial Rp 150.000/bulan"
       atau "Hemat 50%!"

Don't: "Paket Premium Rp 499.000"
Do:    "Paket Premium Rp 499.000 (Rp 16.600/hari — kurang dari harga kopi)"
```

## Price Testing Methods

### Method 1: Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity
Ask 4 questions:
1. "At what price would this be so cheap you'd question quality?" → Floor
2. "At what price is this a bargain — great deal for the value?" → Low
3. "At what price does this start getting expensive but still worth it?" → Target
4. "At what price is this too expensive to consider?" → Ceiling

Plot responses → optimal price is where "bargain" and "expensive" lines cross.

### Method 2: A/B Price Testing
- Show different prices to different visitor segments
- Track conversion rate AND revenue per visitor (not just conversions)
- Higher price with lower conversion can generate MORE revenue
- Run for minimum 2 weeks with 200+ conversions per variant

### Method 3: Grandfather + Raise
- Keep existing customers at old price (grandfather them)
- Raise price for all new customers
- Compare new customer conversion rate to old
- If conversion holds → permanent increase
- If drops significantly → find the right middle ground

## Packaging & Bundling

### Feature Gating Strategy
Decide what's free vs paid based on:
- **Free:** Features that create habit and switching cost
- **Paid:** Features that deliver measurable business value
- **Free trial:** Let them experience the full paid value, then gate

### Bundle Psychology
- Bundles feel like "deals" even at full price
- Name bundles around outcomes, not features
  - ❌ "Pro Plan: 10 users, 50GB, API access"
  - ✅ "Growth Plan: Everything you need to scale"
- Add a "bonus" item to bundles (increases perceived value)
- Annual plans = bundle of 12 months + discount + bonus

## Revenue Optimization Quick Wins

### 10 Things to Try Before You Lower Your Price

1. **Add a guarantee:** "30-day money back" removes risk, not price
2. **Show ROI calculation:** "Pays for itself in 3 days"
3. **Add social proof near price:** "Used by 500+ teams"
4. **Offer annual discount:** 20% off annual (improves cash flow + retention)
5. **Add a bonus:** Free onboarding call, template pack, extended trial
6. **Reframe the price:** "$2.60/day" instead of "$79/month"
7. **Add a higher anchor tier:** Makes your target plan feel affordable
8. **Show "compared to" pricing:** "vs hiring someone at $3,000/month"
9. **Reduce friction, not price:** Better checkout, fewer steps, more payment options
10. **Improve onboarding:** Fast time-to-value justifies any price
