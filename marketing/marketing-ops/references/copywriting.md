## Core Formulas

### PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution
Best for: Ads, emails, short-form content

```
PROBLEM:  Name the pain they feel right now
AGITATE:  Twist the knife — what happens if they DON'T solve it?
SOLUTION: Present your product as the relief
```

**Example:**
> Spending 3 hours every week formatting reports? (Problem)
> That's 156 hours a year — almost a full month of work — wasted on busy work
> while your competitors ship faster. (Agitate)
> ReportBot generates formatted reports in 30 seconds.
> Get your time back. (Solution)

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
Best for: Landing pages, sales pages, long-form

```
ATTENTION: Stop the scroll — bold claim, surprising stat, provocative question
INTEREST:  Make them lean in — "here's how" or "here's why"
DESIRE:    Paint the outcome — what life looks like after
ACTION:    Clear CTA — one specific next step
```

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge
Best for: Case studies, testimonials, transformation stories

```
BEFORE: Where they are now (struggling, frustrated, stuck)
AFTER:  Where they could be (thriving, efficient, successful)
BRIDGE: How your product gets them there
```

### 4P — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push
Best for: Product launches, feature announcements

```
PROMISE: Bold benefit statement
PICTURE: Help them visualize having the benefit
PROOF:   Data, testimonials, case studies
PUSH:    Urgency + CTA
```

### PASTOR — Problem, Amplify, Story, Testimony, Offer, Response
Best for: Long-form sales, webinar pitches, VSLs

```
PROBLEM:   Name and empathize with the pain
AMPLIFY:   Show the consequences of not solving it
STORY:     Tell a relatable story of someone who had the problem
TESTIMONY: Share their transformation and results
OFFER:     Present what you're offering
RESPONSE:  Tell them exactly what to do next
```

### FAB — Feature, Advantage, Benefit
Best for: Product descriptions, feature pages

```
FEATURE:   What the product does (factual)
ADVANTAGE: Why that matters compared to alternatives
BENEFIT:   How it improves their life/work (emotional)
```

**Example:**
> Feature: AI-powered email subject line generator
> Advantage: Tests 50 variants in seconds (vs hours of manual brainstorming)
> Benefit: Open rates up 34% — more people read your emails, more revenue

### QUEST — Qualify, Understand, Educate, Stimulate, Transition
Best for: Educational content, thought leadership, nurture sequences

```
QUALIFY:    Make sure the right audience is reading ("If you manage a team of 5+...")
UNDERSTAND: Show you understand their world
EDUCATE:    Teach something valuable (give before you ask)
STIMULATE:  Create excitement about what's possible
TRANSITION: Naturally bridge to your product/CTA
```

### Star-Chain-Hook
Best for: Social posts, viral threads

```
STAR:  Open with a captivating character, stat, or idea
CHAIN: Build a logical chain of connected facts/arguments
HOOK:  End with a compelling takeaway or CTA
```

## Headline Formulas

### The 30 Headline Patterns That Work

**Number-based:**
1. `{Number} Ways to {Achieve Result} (Without {Common Pain})`
2. `{Number} Mistakes {Audience} Make With {Topic}`
3. `{Number} {Topic} Tips From {Credential}`

**How-to:**
4. `How to {Achieve Result} in {Timeframe}`
5. `How {Company/Person} {Achieved Result} (And How You Can Too)`
6. `How I {Achieved Specific Result} With {Method}`

**Question:**
7. `Are You Making This {Topic} Mistake?`
8. `What If You Could {Dream Outcome}?`
9. `Why Do {Successful People} All {Do This Thing}?`

**Curiosity gap:**
10. `The {Topic} Trick Nobody Talks About`
11. `What {Expert/Company} Knows About {Topic} That You Don't`
12. `The Hidden Reason Your {Thing} Isn't Working`

**Social proof:**
13. `Why {Number} {Audience} Switched to {Product}`
14. `How {Known Brand} {Achieved Result} Using {Method}`
15. `The {Tool/Method} Behind {Impressive Result}`

**Negative:**
16. `Stop {Bad Practice} — Do This Instead`
17. `{Common Advice} Is Wrong. Here's Why.`
18. `Why {Popular Thing} Is Killing Your {Metric}`

**Direct benefit:**
19. `Get {Result} Without {Sacrifice}`
20. `{Result} in {Timeframe} — Guaranteed`
21. `The Fastest Way to {Achieve Result}`

**Comparison:**
22. `{Option A} vs {Option B}: Which Is Right For You?`
23. `{Year} Guide: {Product Category} Compared`
24. `I Tried {Number} {Products} — Here's What Actually Works`

**Urgency:**
25. `Last Chance: {Offer} Ends {Deadline}`
26. `Before You {Action}, Read This`
27. `{Action} Before {Competitor/Market} Does`

**Personal/Story:**
28. `I {Failed/Struggled} Until I {Discovered This}`
29. `What {Time Period} of {Experience} Taught Me About {Topic}`
30. `The Day I Realized {Insight} Changed Everything`

## CTA Formulas

### Button Text Patterns
**Value-first (recommended):**
- "Get My Free {Deliverable}" → "Get My Free Audit"
- "Start {Achieving Result}" → "Start Saving Time"
- "See How It Works"
- "Try It Free — No Card Required"

**Action-first:**
- "Download the Guide"
- "Book My Demo"
- "Start Free Trial"
- "Join {Number}+ {Audience}"

**Urgency (use sparingly, only when genuine):**
- "Claim Your Spot"
- "Get Early Access"
- "Lock In This Price"

### CTA Surrounding Copy
What goes AROUND the button matters as much as the button text:

```
{Benefit statement — what they GET by clicking}
    [CTA Button]
{Friction reducer — "No credit card" / "Cancel anytime" / "2-minute setup"}
```

## Storytelling Frameworks

### Customer Story Arc
```
1. SITUATION: Who they are, what they were doing
2. PROBLEM:   The specific challenge they faced
3. FAILED ATTEMPTS: What they tried that didn't work (builds empathy)
4. DISCOVERY: How they found your product
5. IMPLEMENTATION: How easy/fast it was to start
6. RESULTS:   Specific, measurable outcomes
7. NEW NORMAL: What their life/work looks like now
```

### Founder Story Arc
```
1. FRUSTRATION: "I kept running into this problem..."
2. REALIZATION: "I realized there had to be a better way..."
3. BUILDING:    "So I built..."
4. VALIDATION:  "And when others tried it..."
5. MISSION:     "Now we're on a mission to..."
```

## Copy Editing Checklist

Before publishing ANY copy, run through these:

**Clarity:**
- [ ] Could a 12-year-old understand the main point?
- [ ] Is the benefit clear within the first sentence?
- [ ] Have I removed all unnecessary jargon?
- [ ] Does each paragraph earn its place?

**Power:**
- [ ] Active voice > Passive voice throughout?
- [ ] Specific numbers > vague claims? ("34% faster" not "much faster")
- [ ] Strong verbs > weak verb + adverb? ("sprinted" not "ran quickly")
- [ ] Concrete examples > abstract concepts?

**Structure:**
- [ ] Hook in the first line?
- [ ] One idea per paragraph?
- [ ] Paragraphs under 3 sentences?
- [ ] Scannable with headers/bold for key points?

**Persuasion:**
- [ ] Social proof included?
- [ ] Objections addressed?
- [ ] CTA clear and specific?
- [ ] Urgency present (if genuine)?

**Brand:**
- [ ] Matches brand voice guidelines?
- [ ] Consistent with other brand touchpoints?
- [ ] No words on the "avoid" list?

## Voice & Tone Calibration

### Tone Spectrum
The VOICE stays constant. The TONE adapts to context.

| Context | Tone Dial |
|---------|-----------|
| Error/failure message | Empathetic, calm, solution-focused |
| Product announcement | Excited, clear, benefit-focused |
| Sales email | Confident, specific, value-driven |
| Support response | Warm, patient, thorough |
| Social media | Conversational, witty, human |
| Legal/compliance | Precise, neutral, complete |
| Crisis communication | Transparent, accountable, measured |

### B2B vs B2C Voice Differences

| Dimension | B2B | B2C |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Decision driver | ROI, efficiency, risk reduction | Emotion, identity, convenience |
| Proof type | Case studies, data, ROI calculators | Reviews, UGC, before/after |
| Vocabulary | Industry terms OK if audience expects | Plain language always |
| CTA style | "Book a demo", "Talk to sales" | "Buy now", "Try free", "Add to cart" |
| Content length | Longer, more detailed | Shorter, more visual |
| Humor | Subtle, industry-specific | Broader, more playful |
