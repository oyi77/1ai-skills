# Social Media Optimization (SMO) Reference

SMO is making your content algorithm-friendly so platforms actively distribute it
to people who don't follow you yet. This is the difference between posting and growing.

---

## How Social Algorithms Work (2026)

All major platforms now run **multiple ranking systems** depending on where content appears:
- **Following Feed** — content from accounts you follow (limited organic reach)
- **Discovery Feed** — algorithmic recommendations to non-followers (the growth engine)
- **Search** — keyword/hashtag matching (social SEO)
- **Topic clusters** — AI grouping of related content for recommendations

**Your content can flop in the Following feed but explode in Discovery** — optimize for both.

### The 4 Universal Algorithm Signals

Every platform's algorithm optimizes for these 4 outcomes:

1. **Attention Quality** — not just views, but watch duration, replays, scroll-stops
2. **Satisfaction** — continued watching, fewer "not interested" signals, return visits
3. **Meaningful Engagement** — saves, shares, comments with substance > likes
4. **Session Contribution** — does your content keep people on the platform longer?

## Platform-Specific SMO

### Instagram Algorithm (2026)
**Feed ranking factors:** Relationship, interest prediction, timeliness, session time
**Reels ranking:** Watch time, replay rate, shares, audio page visits
**Explore:** Interest matching, engagement velocity, content freshness

**Optimization:**
- Reels outperform all other formats for reach (2-5x vs static posts)
- First 1-3 seconds determine 80% of performance (hook or die)
- Saves and shares weight more than likes in algorithm
- Carousel posts get 2nd chance in feed (appear again when swiped)
- Post when YOUR audience is active (check Insights → Your Audience)
- Use 5-10 hashtags (mix of discovery + niche)
- Keywords in captions are now searchable — write SEO-friendly captions
- Alt text on images helps accessibility AND algorithm understanding

### TikTok Algorithm (2026)
**Primary signals:** Watch time %, completion rate, shares, rewatches
**Content graph > Social graph** — followers matter less than content quality

**Optimization:**
- Hook in first 1 second (pattern interrupt, text overlay, unexpected visual)
- Ideal length: 30-60 seconds (90s for educational)
- Completion rate is king — shorter videos that get watched fully > long ones
- Shares (send to friend) = strongest signal for viral distribution
- Trending sounds boost initial distribution significantly
- Post 1-3 times per day during testing phase
- Captions should include keywords (TikTok search is growing fast)
- Reply to comments with video (algorithm loves this)

### LinkedIn Algorithm (2026)
**Primary signals:** Dwell time, comment quality, relevance to professional interests
**Penalized:** External links in post, engagement bait, excessive hashtags

**Optimization:**
- Text-only posts and carousels outperform link posts
- First 2 lines must hook (before "See more" fold)
- Dwell time is a ranking factor — write posts worth reading slowly
- Comments in first hour determine post reach trajectory
- Respond to every comment within 1 hour
- Post links in first comment, not in the post body
- 3-5 hashtags maximum
- Personal stories + professional insight = highest engagement formula
- Threads (short) > LinkedIn articles (low distribution)

### YouTube Algorithm (2026)
**Primary signal:** "Viewer satisfaction" — does the viewer want more?
**Measured by:** Watch time, session watch time, return visits

**Optimization:**
- Thumbnail + Title = 80% of click decision
- First 30 seconds must deliver on the title promise
- Retention graph matters: any sharp drop-off hurts recommendations
- End screens + cards drive session continuation
- Chapters (timestamps) help search + user satisfaction
- Longer sessions (playlists, series) = more recommendations
- Consistency in topic builds "topical authority" for recommendations

### Twitter/X Algorithm (2026)
**Shift:** Pay-to-play model — verified users get more distribution
**Primary signals:** Engagement velocity, bookmark rate, long replies

**Optimization:**
- Threads > single tweets for reach
- First tweet = hook; promise value
- Quote tweets distribute to QT author's audience too
- Bookmark rate is a strong quality signal
- Post 3-5 times daily (high-volume platform)
- Engage with others in your niche (give to get)

### Threads Algorithm (2026)
**Focus:** Real-time topic discovery, not follower-based
**Opportunity:** Predicted to surpass X in active users

**Optimization:**
- Engagement within first hour determines distribution
- Conversational, opinion-driven content performs best
- Trending topics get algorithmic boost
- Lower competition = higher organic reach (for now)

## SMO Profile Optimization Checklist

Every social profile should be optimized:

- [ ] **Username** — consistent across all platforms, searchable, brand-aligned
- [ ] **Display name** — includes a keyword if possible ("Maya | Marketing Tips")
- [ ] **Profile photo** — high-res, recognizable at small size (logo or headshot)
- [ ] **Cover/banner** — current, shows value prop or latest campaign
- [ ] **Bio** — clear value proposition, who you help, keywords, CTA
- [ ] **Link** — to current priority page (use link-in-bio tool if needed)
- [ ] **Contact info** — business email, phone, location if relevant
- [ ] **Category/industry** — properly set on platforms that offer it
- [ ] **Highlights/Pinned** — showcase best content at top of profile
- [ ] **CTA button** — configured (Book, Shop, Contact, etc.)

## Content Optimization by Format

### Video Optimization (All Platforms)
- **Hook** in first 1-3 seconds (text overlay + visual disruption + audio hook)
- **Captions always on** — 85% watch without sound
- **Vertical (9:16)** for Reels/TikTok/Shorts, horizontal for YouTube
- **Thumbnail** — face with emotion + bold text + high contrast
- **CTA at end** — but only after delivering value
- **Description** — keyword-rich, include relevant hashtags

### Carousel/Slide Optimization
- **Slide 1** = hook (bold statement or question)
- **8-12 slides** optimal length
- **One idea per slide** — large text, minimal clutter
- **Last slide** = CTA (follow, save, share, comment)
- **Consistent design** — branded template, readable fonts

### Text Post Optimization
- **First line = hook** — must earn the "See more" click
- **Short paragraphs** — 1-2 sentences, lots of white space
- **Formatting** — use line breaks, bullet points (on platforms that support)
- **End with engagement prompt** — question, poll, or CTA

## Engagement Optimization

### The 1-Hour Rule
Engagement in the **first 60 minutes** determines whether the algorithm
distributes your content widely. Plan for it:

1. Post when your audience is most active
2. Respond to every comment within 15 minutes
3. Ask a question in the post to prompt immediate comments
4. Share the post to Stories (Instagram) or repost channels
5. Engage with 10-15 other posts in your niche right before/after posting

### Engagement Signal Hierarchy (Most to Least Valuable)
```
1. SHARES / SENDS — "I want others to see this" (strongest growth signal)
2. SAVES / BOOKMARKS — "I want to return to this" (strongest quality signal)
3. COMMENTS — "I want to discuss this" (engagement depth)
4. WATCH TIME — "I want to keep watching" (content quality)
5. LIKES / REACTIONS — "I acknowledge this" (lowest signal value)
```

Optimize for shares and saves, not likes.

## Social SEO

### How Social Search Works
Social platforms are now search engines. Users search inside Instagram, TikTok,
YouTube, and LinkedIn — not just Google.

**Optimization for social search:**
- Include primary keyword in caption/description (naturally)
- Use keyword variations in hashtags
- Add keywords to profile bio
- Name your content files descriptively (not IMG_0001.jpg)
- Add alt text to all images
- Use closed captions on videos (AI reads the transcript)

### Keyword Research for Social
- Search your topic on each platform — see what autocompletes
- Check "Related searches" and "People also searched" sections
- Monitor trending hashtags in your niche weekly
- Use platform-native analytics to see which search terms find you
