# Customer Research Reference

Don't build what you THINK customers want. Build what they TELL you they need.
This reference covers research methods a solo founder can run without a team.

---

## Research Methods for Solo Founders

### The Research Stack (No Team Required)

| Method | Time | Cost | Best For |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| Review mining | 30 min | Free | Understanding language, pain points, objections |
| Social listening | Ongoing | Free | Real-time sentiment, trends, competitor gaps |
| Customer interviews | 30 min each | Free | Deep understanding, persona validation |
| Surveys | 1 hour setup | Free-$50 | Quantitative validation at scale |
| Analytics analysis | 30 min | Free | Behavior patterns, funnel bottlenecks |
| Competitor review analysis | 1 hour | Free | What customers love/hate about alternatives |
| Jobs-to-be-Done interviews | 45 min each | Free | Understanding purchase motivation |

## Review Mining

### Where to Mine Reviews
- **Your competitors' reviews:** G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Google Reviews
- **App stores:** Google Play, App Store (for app competitors)
- **Marketplaces:** Tokopedia, Shopee, Amazon reviews
- **Social mentions:** Reddit, Twitter, Facebook groups
- **Forum discussions:** Quora, Stack Exchange, niche forums

### What to Extract
```
For each review, capture:
1. PAIN POINT: What problem drove them to look for a solution?
2. LANGUAGE: Exact words they use to describe the problem
3. DESIRED OUTCOME: What they hoped to achieve
4. WHAT THEY TRIED: Previous solutions and why they failed
5. WHAT THEY LOVE: Features/aspects that delighted them
6. WHAT THEY HATE: Frustrations and unmet needs
7. OBJECTIONS: Concerns about purchasing/switching
```

### Review Mining Template
```markdown
## Review Mining: {Competitor Name}
Source: {G2/Capterra/etc.}
Date: {date}
Reviews analyzed: {count}

### Top 5 Pain Points (by frequency)
1. "{exact customer words}" — mentioned {X} times
2. "{exact customer words}" — mentioned {X} times
...

### Top 5 Loved Features
1. "{what they love}" — mentioned {X} times
...

### Top 5 Complaints
1. "{what they hate}" — mentioned {X} times
...

### Language Patterns (Use in your copy)
- Customers say "{their words}" not "{your jargon}"
- Common phrases: "{pattern 1}", "{pattern 2}"

### Opportunities (Gaps you can fill)
1. {Unmet need competitors don't address}
2. {Feature everyone complains about}
```

## Customer Interviews

### The 5-Question Interview (30 Minutes)

```
1. "Tell me about the last time you dealt with {problem}."
   → Understand the context and emotional state

2. "Walk me through what you did to try to solve it."
   → Understand their journey, what they've tried, and why it failed

3. "What was the hardest part about that?"
   → Find the real pain point (often different from what they initially say)

4. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be?"
   → Understand their ideal outcome

5. "What would you be willing to pay for that solution?"
   → Validate willingness to pay (ask at the end, after they've articulated value)
```

### Interview Rules
- **Listen 80%, talk 20%** — your job is to learn, not pitch
- **Ask about past behavior**, not hypothetical future ("Tell me about the last time" not "Would you use...")
- **Follow the energy** — when they get animated about something, dig deeper
- **Don't lead the witness** — "What do you think about X?" not "Don't you think X is a problem?"
- **Record everything** — ask permission, use Otter.ai or just voice memos
- **5 interviews reveal 85% of issues** — you don't need hundreds

### Finding Interview Subjects
- Existing customers (best source)
- Free trial users who didn't convert (goldmine — they're experiencing the problem)
- People in relevant communities (Reddit, Facebook groups, WhatsApp groups)
- LinkedIn outreach to your ICP
- Offer $20 coffee/lunch or product credit for their time

## Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) Framework

### The JTBD Interview
People don't buy products. They "hire" products to do a job.

**The Timeline:**
```
FIRST THOUGHT          PASSIVE LOOKING        ACTIVE LOOKING         DECIDING
"I have a problem"  →  "What's out there?"  →  "Comparing options"  →  "Making the switch"
     ↓                      ↓                       ↓                      ↓
What triggered it?    Where did they look?    What criteria mattered?  What pushed them over?
```

**Key JTBD Questions:**
1. "When did you first realize you needed something like this?"
2. "What were you using before? What made you start looking for alternatives?"
3. "What other options did you consider? Why did you choose us (or not)?"
4. "What almost stopped you from buying?"
5. "What's different about your life/work now that you have this?"

### JTBD Statement Template
```
When I {situation/context},
I want to {motivation/desired progress},
so I can {expected outcome}.
```

**Example:**
"When I'm managing 5 client projects simultaneously,
I want to see all deadlines and status in one place,
so I can stop worrying about missing something important."

## Surveys

### When to Use Surveys (vs Interviews)
- **Surveys:** Validate what you learned from interviews at scale
- **Interviews:** Discover things you didn't know to ask about
- Rule: Interview first (5-10 people), then survey (50-500 people)

### Survey Design Rules
- Maximum 5-7 questions (completion rate drops 20% per additional question)
- One question per screen on mobile
- Mix of multiple choice and one open-ended
- Start easy, end with the important question
- Always include "Other" option for multiple choice
- Test on mobile before sending

### The 5 Survey Questions That Matter
```
1. "What's the #1 challenge you face with {problem area}?"
   [Open-ended — discover language and priorities]

2. "How are you currently handling this?"
   [Multiple choice: Manual, Competitor A, Competitor B, Nothing, Other]

3. "How important is solving this on a scale of 1-5?"
   [1-5 scale — filter for high-priority respondents]

4. "If a solution existed that {value prop}, how likely would you be to try it?"
   [1-5 scale — measure purchase intent]

5. "What would make you hesitate to try a new solution?"
   [Open-ended — discover objections to address in marketing]
```

### Survey Distribution
- Email to existing users/subscribers
- Post in relevant communities (with permission)
- WhatsApp broadcast to contacts
- Social media post with link
- In-app prompt for active users
- Offer incentive: entry to draw, discount code, early access

## Competitive Voice-of-Customer Analysis

### Quick Competitive Research (1 Hour)

```
For each top competitor:
1. Read 20 recent G2/Capterra reviews (10 positive, 10 negative)
2. Extract: what customers love, what they hate, what's missing
3. Search Reddit for "{competitor name} alternative"
4. Search Twitter for "{competitor name} sucks" or "{competitor} problem"
5. Compile into opportunity matrix
```

### Opportunity Matrix
```
| Need | Competitor A | Competitor B | You | Opportunity |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----|-------------|
| {need 1} | ✅ Strong | ❌ Weak | ❌ Gap | BUILD THIS |
| {need 2} | ✅ Strong | ✅ Strong | ❌ Gap | MATCH THIS |
| {need 3} | ❌ Weak | ❌ Weak | — | DIFFERENTIATE HERE |
| {need 4} | ✅ Strong | ✅ Strong | ✅ Strong | TABLE STAKES |
```

**Focus on needs where competitors are weak AND demand is high.**
