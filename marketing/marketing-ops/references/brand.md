## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Voice Discovery Process

Guide the user through these questions to define their brand voice:

**Personality:**
- If your brand were a person, how would they speak?
- What 3 adjectives describe your brand's personality?
- What 3 adjectives your brand is NOT?

**Audience:**
- Who are you talking to? (demographics, psychographics, sophistication level)
- How does your audience talk to each other? (formal/casual, jargon/plain)
- What do they care about most? (efficiency, creativity, status, savings)

**Differentiators:**
- What do you want people to feel after interacting with your brand?
- What makes your voice different from competitors?
- What's your "hot take" or contrarian view in your industry?

```markdown
# {Brand Name} Voice Guidelines

## Voice Attributes
Our voice is: **{Attribute 1}**, **{Attribute 2}**, **{Attribute 3}**
Our voice is NOT: **{Anti-1}**, **{Anti-2}**, **{Anti-3}**

{The voice stays consistent, but tone adapts to context:}

|---------|----------------|
```

## Messaging Framework

### Message Architecture

```markdown
# {Brand} Messaging Framework

## Brand Promise
{One sentence: What you commit to delivering}

## Positioning Statement
For {target audience} who {need/want},
{Brand} is the {category} that {key benefit}
unlike {alternatives} because {unique differentiator}.

## Value Propositions (by Audience Segment)

### Segment 1: {Name}
- **Primary pain:** {Their biggest frustration}
- **Key message:** {How you solve it}
- **Proof point:** {Evidence it works}
- **CTA:** {What you want them to do}

### Segment 2: {Name}
{Same structure}

## Elevator Pitches

### 10-second version
"{Brand} helps {audience} {achieve outcome} by {mechanism}."

### 30-second version
"{Audience} struggle with {problem}. Most solutions {why alternatives fail}.
{Brand} takes a different approach: {unique mechanism}. The result?
{Specific outcome with proof point}."

### 2-minute version
{Expanded with story, data, and examples}

## Message Hierarchy
1. **Lead with:** {The single most compelling benefit}
2. **Support with:** {Secondary benefits, in priority order}
3. **Prove with:** {Data, testimonials, case studies}
4. **Close with:** {Clear CTA and risk reversal}

## Key Claims (with Evidence)
| Claim | Evidence | Where to Use |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| "{Specific claim}" | {Data/proof} | {Channels/contexts} |
```

## Tagline Generation

### Tagline Frameworks
- **Benefit-driven:** "{Outcome} made {adjective}" — "Growth made simple"
- **Action-driven:** "{Verb} your {noun}" — "Unlock your potential"
- **Challenge-driven:** "Stop {pain}. Start {gain}." — "Stop guessing. Start growing."
- **Identity-driven:** "For {who} who {aspiration}" — "For builders who ship"
- **Contrast-driven:** "{Not X}. {Y}." — "Not just analytics. Answers."
- **Metaphor-driven:** "{Creative metaphor for value}" — "Your marketing co-pilot"

### Generation Process
1. Start with 20+ rough tagline ideas (quantity first)
2. Filter by: memorable, differentiating, true, simple
3. Shortlist to 5
4. Test each against: "Would someone put this on a t-shirt?"
5. Check for unintended meanings, translations, competitor overlap
6. Present top 3 with rationale

## Brand Story

### Brand Narrative Structure

**Origin Story (Why you exist)**
```
{Founder/team} was frustrated by {problem}.
They tried {existing solutions} but found {why they fell short}.
So they built {product/company} with a simple belief: {core belief}.
Today, {brand} helps {audience} {achieve outcome}.
```

**Mission Statement**
- What you do + For whom + Why it matters
- Keep it under 20 words
- Example: "We empower small businesses to compete with enterprises through AI-powered marketing tools."

**Vision Statement**
- The world you're trying to create
- Aspirational but believable
- Example: "A world where every business, regardless of size, has access to world-class marketing."

**Values (3-5 max)**
Each value should be:
- Actionable (not just a word, but a behavior)
- Differentiating (not generic like "integrity" — everyone says that)
- True (evidenced by actual behavior)

Format: **{Value Name}** — {One sentence explaining what it means in practice}

## Brand Audit

### Brand Consistency Checklist

**Visual Identity**
- [ ] Logo used consistently across all touchpoints
- [ ] Color palette consistent (check hex codes)
- [ ] Typography consistent (fonts, sizes, hierarchy)
- [ ] Image style consistent (photography, illustration, iconography)
- [ ] Templates exist for common assets (social, presentations, email)

**Voice & Messaging**
- [ ] Brand voice guidelines documented and accessible
- [ ] Messaging consistent across website, social, email, ads
- [ ] Tone appropriate per channel (formal on LinkedIn, casual on Twitter)
- [ ] Value proposition clear and consistent
- [ ] Boilerplate text up to date (about page, social bios, email signatures)

**Digital Presence**
- [ ] Website reflects current branding
- [ ] Social profiles consistent (bios, images, links)
- [ ] Email templates match brand guidelines
- [ ] Ad creative follows brand guidelines
- [ ] Third-party listings accurate (Google Business, directories)

**Customer Experience**
- [ ] Onboarding reflects brand personality
- [ ] Support communications on-brand
- [ ] Error messages and notifications on-brand
- [ ] Packaging and physical materials consistent (if applicable)

### Audit Output: Report with screenshots, inconsistencies flagged,
and prioritized recommendations for alignment.
