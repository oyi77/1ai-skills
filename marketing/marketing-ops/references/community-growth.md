# Community-Led Growth & Dark Social Reference

84% of online sharing happens in private, untrackable channels.
Community-influenced deals close 72% within 90 days vs 42% for marketing-led.
This is where buying decisions actually happen.

## Community-Led Growth (CLG)

### The CLG Ladder
```
LURKER → PARTICIPANT → CONTRIBUTOR → ADVOCATE → AMBASSADOR
  Read      Comment        Create        Promote      Lead
  Watch     Ask questions  Answer others Refer users  Moderate
  Learn     React          Share expertise Testimonials Co-create
```

### Where to Build Community (Pick ONE to start)

| Platform | Best For | Effort | Control |
|----------|----------|--------|---------|
| Discord | Developer/tech communities | High | High |
| WhatsApp Group | Indonesia/LatAm, local | Low | Medium |
| Telegram Group | Tech, crypto, international | Medium | Medium |
| Reddit (subreddit) | Niche topics, SEO value | Medium | Low |
| Facebook Group | Consumer, local business, older demo | Medium | Low |
| Slack | B2B professional communities | Medium | High |
| Circle/Skool | Paid communities, courses | Medium | High |

### Community-Led Growth for Solo Founders

**Month 1: Plant Seeds**
- Join 5-10 communities where your ICP hangs out
- Contribute value daily: answer questions, share insights
- NO selling, NO product promotion — just be genuinely helpful
- Track which communities drive the most profile visits

**Month 2: Build Reputation**
- Become a recognized name in 2-3 communities
- Share original insights and mini case studies
- Start mentioning your product naturally when it solves a specific question
- Collect DMs from people who want to learn more

**Month 3: Create Your Own**
- Start a small community (Discord/WhatsApp/Telegram)
- Seed with 20-50 people from your existing network
- Set clear purpose: "A group for {ICP} to {shared goal}"
- Post valuable content 3-5x per week
- Never let it become a product promotion channel

## Reddit Marketing Playbook

### The Rules (Violate These = Banned)
- No direct product promotion (ever)
- Be a human first, founder second
- 90% value, 10% subtle mentions (at most)
- Never use multiple accounts to upvote yourself
- Read subreddit rules before posting (every sub is different)

### The Proven Playbook (One founder: $0 to $17K MRR in 4 months)

**Step 1: Find Your Subreddits**
- Search for subreddits where your ICP discusses the problem you solve
- Look for: 10K-500K members, active daily posts, relevant discussions
- Create a list of 10-30 target subreddits

**Step 2: Provide Pure Value (Weeks 1-4)**
- Answer questions thoroughly — longer, more detailed than anyone else
- Share frameworks, templates, checklists (not product links)
- Comment on existing posts with genuine insights
- Post original content: how-tos, analyses, comparisons

**Step 3: Strategic Mentioning (Weeks 5+)**
- When someone asks exactly the question your product answers:
  Answer the question fully FIRST, then mention: "I also built {product} for this"
- Share "Show Reddit" posts about your journey (like Show HN)
- Post genuine case studies from customers (with permission)

**Step 4: Track & Scale**
- Which subreddits drive traffic? (UTM: ?utm_source=reddit&utm_medium=organic)
- Which post types get engagement? (how-tos vs opinions vs tools)
- Scale what works: post more in winning subreddits

## Building in Public (BIP)

### Why BIP Works for Solo Founders
- Builds audience before you have a product to sell
- Creates accountability (public commitments)
- Generates authentic content effortlessly
- Attracts customers, partners, advisors, investors
- Makes your brand human (people buy from people)

### What to Share
```
✅ Revenue milestones ("Hit $1K MRR today!")
✅ Monthly revenue reports (transparent numbers)
✅ Product decisions ("We're building X because customers said Y")
✅ Failures and learnings ("This campaign flopped. Here's why...")
✅ Behind-the-scenes (design process, code, planning)
✅ Customer wins (with permission)
✅ Metrics and data (engagement, conversion, growth rates)

❌ Competitor bashing
❌ Vague motivational posts without substance
❌ Only sharing wins (not credible)
❌ Customer private data or confidential info
```

### BIP Content Calendar
```
Monday:    Weekly metrics update (MRR, users, key numbers)
Tuesday:   Product/feature update (what you're building)
Wednesday: Learning/insight from this week
Thursday:  Customer story or feedback highlight
Friday:    Question for your audience (engagement)
```

### Best Platforms for BIP
- **Twitter/X** — fastest feedback loop, tech/startup audience
- **LinkedIn** — B2B audience, professional credibility
- **IndieHackers** — founder-specific community
- **YouTube** — long-form journey documentation
- **Personal blog** — SEO value, owned platform

## Dark Social Strategy

### What Is Dark Social
Content shared privately where you can't track the source:
- WhatsApp forwards
- Slack DMs
- Email forwards
- Telegram messages
- Instagram DMs
- Text messages

**These show up in analytics as "Direct" traffic — but they're actually referrals.**

### Designing Content for Dark Social

Content that gets forwarded privately is:
1. **Specific and practical** — "Here's the exact template I use for X"
2. **Saves the recipient time** — checklists, calculators, templates
3. **Makes the sharer look smart** — "Look what I found"
4. **Emotionally resonant** — stories that make people think "me too"
5. **Easy to summarize** — the key insight survives being described in a message

### Dark Social Content Formats
| Format | Why It Gets Shared Privately |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Templates and checklists | "Here, use this" |
| Calculator tools | "Try this — enter your numbers" |
| Hot take / contrarian insight | "Can you believe this?" |
| Salary/pricing data | "Look at these numbers" |
| Behind-the-scenes stories | "This is how they actually do it" |
| Free resources / guides | "You need to download this" |

### Measuring Dark Social
Since you can't track it directly:
1. **"How did you hear about us?"** survey — add to signup, purchase, demo
2. **Track "Direct" traffic spikes** — correlate with content publishing
3. **Monitor brand search volume** — increases = dark social awareness
4. **Ask in sales calls** — "What made you reach out today?"
5. **Self-reported attribution** — let users tell you (open text field)

## Founder-Led Marketing

### Why Founder Content Gets 8x More Engagement Than Brand Content

People follow people, not logos. On LinkedIn, employee/founder posts get
8x more engagement than brand page posts. Use this asymmetry.

### The Solo Founder Content Strategy
```
Post from YOUR personal account, not the company page.
Your face. Your name. Your voice. Your story.

Content mix:
  40% — Insights from building (lessons, data, decisions)
  30% — Helpful content (tips, frameworks, how-tos)
  20% — Customer/industry stories
  10% — Product updates (subtle, value-framed)
```

### Building Your Founder Brand
1. Pick ONE platform where your ICP is most active
2. Post 3-5x per week consistently for 90 days
3. Engage with 10 other people's posts daily (give to get)
4. Share real numbers, real decisions, real learnings
5. After 90 days: evaluate followers, engagement, leads generated
6. If working → double down. If not → switch platforms.
