# Sales Enablement Reference (Solo Founder Edition)

You don't have a sales team. You ARE the sales team. This reference turns
AI into your always-on sales partner — qualifying leads, writing proposals,
handling objections, and closing deals while you build product.

## Lead Qualification (Automated)

### Auto-Qualify Framework
When a lead comes in (form, email, DM, WhatsApp), evaluate:

```
SCORE EACH 1-5:

NEED:    Do they have the problem you solve?
  5 = Expressed specific pain you address
  3 = Related industry/role, unclear pain
  1 = No apparent need

BUDGET:  Can they pay?
  5 = Stated budget or company size suggests ability
  3 = Unknown but possible
  1 = Student/free-seeker/competitor

TIMING:  Are they ready now?
  5 = "Need this by next week"
  3 = "Exploring options"
  1 = "Just researching for next year"

FIT:     Are they your ideal customer?
  5 = Matches ICP exactly
  3 = Adjacent to ICP
  1 = Wrong market/size/industry

TOTAL: /20
  16-20 → HOT: Respond within 1 hour. Personal outreach.
  10-15 → WARM: Respond within 24 hours. Nurture sequence.
  5-9   → COLD: Auto-nurture. Don't spend personal time.
  <5    → DISQUALIFY: Polite decline or redirect.
```

### Qualification Questions (For Forms/Chat)
Ask these to auto-score leads:
1. "What's the main challenge you're trying to solve?" → NEED
2. "What's your timeline for implementing a solution?" → TIMING
3. "How many people/users would need this?" → BUDGET proxy
4. "What are you currently using to handle this?" → FIT + urgency

## Outbound Sales (AI-Drafted)

### Cold Email Sequence (3 Touches)

**Email 1 — Value-First Introduction**
```
Subject: Quick thought on {their specific situation}

Hi {Name},

I noticed {specific observation about their business — shows homework}.

Many {their role/industry} struggle with {problem you solve}.
We built {product} specifically for this — {one-line value prop}.

{Customer name} saw {specific result} within {timeframe}.

Worth a 15-minute call this week?

{Your name}
```

**Email 2 — Follow-up (3 days later, reply to same thread)**
```
Hi {Name},

Quick follow-up — wanted to share {specific resource: case study, tool, insight}
that might be useful regardless of whether we connect.

{1-2 sentence insight that provides genuine value}

If timing is off, no pressure. Just reply "later" and I'll check back in a month.

{Your name}
```

**Email 3 — Breakup (5 days later)**
```
Hi {Name},

I'll keep this short — I've reached out twice and don't want to be a nuisance.

If {problem} isn't a priority right now, totally understand.

If it is, here's my calendar link: {link}
Either way, wishing you well with {their business/project}.

{Your name}
```

## Demo & Pitch Framework

### The 15-Minute Demo Script
You don't have time for hour-long sales calls. Keep demos tight:

```
[0-2 min]  RAPPORT
  "Thanks for making time. Before I show you anything,
  tell me — what's the biggest challenge you're facing with {problem}?"

[2-5 min]  DIAGNOSE
  Listen. Ask follow-up questions. Understand THEIR situation.
  "How are you handling this today?"
  "What's that costing you in time/money?"
  "What would ideal look like?"

[5-10 min] SHOW (Not Tell)
  Demo only the features that solve THEIR specific problem.
  Don't do a feature tour. Show their use case.
  "Based on what you told me, let me show you exactly how this works..."

[10-13 min] PROOF
  Share 1-2 results from similar customers.
  "A company similar to yours saw {result} in {timeframe}."

[13-15 min] NEXT STEP
  "Based on what you've seen, does this solve the problem?"
  If yes → "Great. Here's how to get started: {specific next step}"
  If maybe → "What would you need to see to feel confident?"
  If no → "Appreciate your honesty. What IS the priority right now?"
```

### The Async Pitch (For When You Can't Do Live Demos)
Record a 5-minute Loom video personalized to the prospect:
1. Open with their name and something specific about their business
2. Show 2-3 features relevant to their situation
3. Share a quick result from a similar customer
4. End with a specific CTA (calendar link, trial link)

## Objection Handling

### The 10 Most Common Objections (With Responses)

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe as ROI: "It costs {X}/month. If it saves you {Y} hours, that's {Z} per hour — less than hiring someone." |
| "I need to think about it" | "Totally fair. What specific concerns do you want to think through? I might be able to address them now." |
| "We're using {competitor}" | "What's working well? What's not? [Listen]. That's exactly why {your advantage}." |
| "Not the right time" | "When would be? I'll set a reminder and follow up then. No pressure." |
| "I need to ask my boss/partner" | "Makes sense. Want me to put together a one-pager they can review? Or I could join a quick call with them." |
| "Can you do a discount?" | "I can offer {annual discount / extended trial / bonus feature} — but the price reflects the value. {Customer} saw {ROI result}." |
| "Is it hard to set up?" | "Here's what onboarding looks like: {3 simple steps}. Most customers are running within {timeframe}." |
| "What if it doesn't work?" | "{Money-back guarantee / free trial period / pilot program}. You risk nothing." |
| "We're too small for this" | "Actually, {product} was built for teams your size. {Small customer example + result}." |
| "Send me more info" | "Happy to — what specifically would be most useful? [Send targeted info, not a generic deck]." |

### Objection Response Formula
```
ACKNOWLEDGE → QUESTION → REFRAME → EVIDENCE → NEXT STEP

"I totally understand that concern." (Acknowledge)
"Can I ask what's driving that?" (Question — dig deeper)
"The way I'd think about it is..." (Reframe)
"{Customer} had the same concern, and they found..." (Evidence)
"Would it help if we...?" (Next step)
```

## Proposal & Pricing

### Proposal Template (1-Page)
```markdown
# Proposal: {Product} for {Their Company}

## The Challenge
{Their specific problem in their words — shows you listened}

## The Solution
{How your product solves it — 3-4 bullet points, benefits not features}

## Expected Results
{Specific outcomes based on similar customers}

## Investment
{Pricing with clear breakdown}
- {Plan name}: {price}/month
- Includes: {key items}
- Setup: {one-time fee if any, or "Free onboarding included"}

## Timeline
- Day 1: Account setup and onboarding
- Week 1: {First milestone}
- Month 1: {Expected first results}

## Next Step
{Exactly what they need to do: "Reply 'go' to get started" or "Sign up at {link}"}

## Guarantee
{Risk reversal: "30-day money-back, no questions asked"}
```

### Pricing Presentation Rules
- Show 3 tiers (Anchor → Target → Entry)
- Highlight the middle tier as "Most Popular"
- Show annual savings prominently
- Add social proof next to pricing ("Join 500+ customers")
- Include guarantee near the price (reduces perceived risk)
- Calculate ROI for them: "Pays for itself in {X} days"

## Follow-Up System

### The Follow-Up Cadence
After any sales conversation, follow this automated sequence:

```
Day 0:  Thank-you email + recap of what was discussed
Day 2:  Relevant resource (case study, blog post, tool)
Day 5:  "Any questions?" check-in
Day 10: Social proof (new testimonial, milestone, or result)
Day 15: Deadline or incentive (if genuine)
Day 21: "Checking in" — brief, no pressure
Day 30: "Breakup" — last touch, leave door open
```

### Follow-Up Principles
- Always add value in every touch (never just "checking in" with no content)
- Reference their specific situation (not generic blasts)
- Vary the channel (email → LinkedIn → WhatsApp → email)
- Stop after 5-7 touches if no response — respect their silence
- Track everything in CRM — never duplicate outreach

## Revenue Metrics for Solo Founders

### Track These Weekly
| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Pipeline value | Sum of all open deals × probability | 3× monthly revenue target |
| Lead velocity | New qualified leads this week vs last | Growing week-over-week |
| Response time | Avg time from lead in to first reply | < 1 hour for hot leads |
| Conversion rate | Deals closed ÷ Deals created | 20-30% for warm leads |
| Average deal size | Total revenue ÷ Number of deals | Growing or stable |
| Sales cycle length | Days from first contact to close | Shrinking over time |
| Revenue per hour spent on sales | Revenue ÷ Hours on sales activities | Track to optimize time allocation |

### The Solo Founder Revenue Formula
```
Monthly Revenue = Traffic × Lead Rate × Qualification Rate × Close Rate × Avg Deal Size

Example:
  5,000 visitors × 3% lead rate = 150 leads
  150 leads × 40% qualified = 60 qualified leads
  60 qualified × 20% close rate = 12 customers
  12 customers × $79/mo = $948/month MRR

To hit $5,000 MRR:
  Need 63 customers at $79/mo
  Need 5.3 new customers/month
  Need 26 qualified leads/month
  Need 65 total leads/month
  Need 2,167 visitors/month (at 3% lead rate)
```
