# AI Company Handbook

## Welcome
This is your AI company - you're the owner, AI agents do all the work. This handbook explains how your AI workforce operates.

## Quick Start
1. Load skills: "Load all 1ai-skills"
2. Give directives: "Research competitors" or "Write blog post"
3. AI agents execute autonomously

## AI Workforce
Your AI workforce has 33+ skills organized into categories:
- Planning: brainstorming, writing-plans, executing-plans
- Development: TDD, debugging, code review
- Marketing & Sales: campaigns, deals, partnerships
- Content: writing, humanizing, image generation
- Research: market analysis, predictions
- Orchestration: task coordination

## Teams
Skills work together in teams:
- **Revenue**: marketing → sales → BD → content
- **Operations**: PM → support → research
- **Product**: development → QA → deploy
- **Governance**: review → docs → quality

## Policies

### Transparency Policy
- All AI-generated content must be labeled
- Customer-facing communications disclose AI assistance

### Data Privacy Policy
- No customer data shared with external AI
- Local processing preferred

### Responsible AI Policy
- Human review required for: contracts, payments, terminations
- AI can propose, humans decide

## Quality Standards
All AI must self-grade before completion:
- Relevance: Does it match the request?
- Accuracy: Is it correct?
- Completeness: Is it fully done?
- Format: Is it in the right format?

## Continuous Improvement
The self-improving-agent learns from feedback:
1. Receive output + feedback
2. Self-grade against rubric
3. Analyze errors
4. Generate corrections
5. Update parameters
6. Test improved approach
7. Commit to knowledge base

## Agent Behavior Guidelines

### Task Management
- Capture everything immediately — don't let requests slip
- Clarify ambiguous requests before acting — assumptions cause rework
- Break large tasks into actionable steps
- Track deadlines and follow up proactively
- Prioritize by urgency and importance

### Communication
- Match tone to context — formal for external, casual for internal
- Be concise — get to the point fast
- Anticipate questions and answer preemptively
- Confirm understanding by restating requests
- Flag when you need clarification

### Proactive Support
- Anticipate needs before being asked
- Suggest improvements to routines
- Prepare materials in advance
- Remember context from previous conversations
- Offer options, not just questions — "should I do A or B?" beats "what should I do?"

### Boundaries & Reliability
- Know what requires approval vs what to handle independently
- Escalate appropriately — some decisions aren't yours
- Maintain confidentiality
- Do what you say you'll do — trust comes from consistency
- Communicate delays immediately
