## Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA)

### Classic Portfolio Frameworks
```
60/40 Portfolio (Traditional):
  60% Equities | 40% Fixed Income
  Expected return: 7–8% nominal | Volatility: 10–12%
  Issue: Bond-equity correlation broke down in 2022 (both down simultaneously)

All-Weather (Ray Dalio / Bridgewater):
  30% Stocks | 40% Long Bonds | 15% Int Bonds | 7.5% Gold | 7.5% Commodities
  Expected return: 6–7% | Volatility: 8–10%
  Strength: Designed for all economic environments

Yale Endowment (David Swensen Model):
  20% Domestic Equity | 15% International | 10% Fixed Income
  25% Private Equity | 20% Real Assets | 10% Absolute Return
  Note: Not directly replicable for most — illiquidity premium requires scale

Risk Parity:
  Equal risk contribution from each asset class (not equal capital)
  Each asset leveraged/de-leveraged to equal volatility contribution
  Works in falling rate environments; struggles when rates rise fast
```

### Factor Tilts (Smart Beta)
- **Value**: Low P/B, P/E stocks — long-run premium, cyclical underperformance
- **Momentum**: Recent 12mo winners — strong factor, crashes sharply in reversals
- **Quality**: High ROE, low debt — defensive factor, consistent premium
- **Low Volatility**: Low beta stocks — anomaly: outperforms despite lower risk
- **Size**: Small cap premium — highly illiquid, requires patience
- **Profitability**: High gross profits/assets — Fama-French 5-factor

  → ISM Manufacturing >50 and rising

  → Yield curve deep inversion (3m10y < –100bps)
  → ISM <45 and falling
  → Fed hiking into slowing growth
  → Earnings revisions negative (>60% companies cutting)
  → VIX spike >30 (forced de-risking)

REGIME DETECTION:
  → GROWTH + LOW INFLATION: Equities, growth stocks
  → GROWTH + HIGH INFLATION: Commodities, TIPS, energy
  → RECESSION + LOW INFLATION: Long bonds, cash, defensive
  → RECESSION + HIGH INFLATION (Stagflation): Gold, commodities, TIPS, energy

## Tax-Efficient Investing

### Tax-Loss Harvesting (TLH)
```
Mechanics:
  1. Sell position at a loss
  2. Immediately buy similar (not substantially identical) replacement
  3. Realize tax loss → offset gains or up to $3,000 ordinary income/yr (US)
  4. After 31-day wash sale window, can buy back original if desired

TLH Value Formula:
  After-Tax Value = Tax Rate × Unrealized Loss (harvested now)
  Real value: Tax deferral + reinvestment of tax savings

Best practices:
  → Harvest throughout year, not just December
  → Use ETF pairs (SPY → VOO) for market exposure continuity
  → Beware wash sale rule for IRAs (cross-account wash sale trap)
  → Track cost basis carefully (FIFO vs. specific ID — use specific ID)
```

### Asset Location Strategy (Maximize After-Tax Return)
```
Tax-Advantaged (401k, IRA, Roth IRA):
  → High-yield bonds (taxed as ordinary income)
  → REITs (ordinary income distributions)
  → Commodities (K-1 complexity)
  → High-turnover active funds
  → Alternatives with high ordinary income

Taxable Account:
  → Municipal bonds (tax-free interest)
  → Buy-and-hold equities (long-term capital gains)
  → Index funds (tax-efficient, low turnover)
  → ETFs (in-kind creation/redemption minimizes capital gains)
  → Direct indexing (TLH on individual positions)

Roth IRA (Best):
  → Highest expected return assets (growth stocks, PE equivalents)
  → Assets you'll hold for 30+ years
  → Assets expected to appreciate most
```

## Estate Planning

### Key Vehicles
```
Revocable Living Trust:
  → Avoids probate, maintains privacy
  → Fully revocable during lifetime
  → No estate tax benefits

Irrevocable Trust (ILIT, GRAT, SLAT, QPRT):
  → Removes assets from taxable estate
  → No control after transfer
  → Grantor Retained Annuity Trust (GRAT): Transfer appreciation gift-tax free

Annual Gift Tax Exclusion (2025):
  → $18,000/person/year (no gift tax)
  → Married couple: $36,000/person/year
  → Direct medical/tuition payments: Unlimited (paid directly to institution)

Lifetime Exemption (2025):
  → $13.61M per person (sunsets to ~$7M after 2025 without legislation)
  → Portability: Deceased spouse unused exemption (DSUE)

529 Plans (Education):
  → Tax-free growth if used for qualified education expenses
  → Superfunding: 5-year front-load ($90,000 lump sum in year 1)
  → Rollover to Roth IRA: Up to $35,000 lifetime (if 529 ≥15 years old)
```

### UHNW (Ultra High Net Worth) Structures
1. **Family Limited Partnership (FLP)**: Valuation discounts (20–40%) for gifting
2. **Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT)**: Income stream now, charity gets remainder
3. **Donor Advised Fund (DAF)**: Immediate deduction, grant over time
4. **Private Foundation**: Maximum control, but complex compliance
5. **Qualified Opportunity Zones (QOZ)**: Defer and reduce capital gains + tax-free appreciation
6. **QSBS (Sec. 1202)**: Exclude up to $10M (or 10× basis) in startup gains

## Retirement Planning

### Withdrawal Rate Strategy
```
4% Rule (Bengen Rule):
  → $1M portfolio → $40,000/year
  → 30-year historical success rate: 95%+
  → Caveat: Low current yields + high valuations may reduce to 3–3.5% safe

Dynamic Withdrawal Strategies:
  → Guardrails method: Raise/lower spending based on portfolio performance
  → Bucket strategy: Year 1–3 in cash, Year 4–10 in bonds, Year 10+ in equities
  → Floor-and-upside: Annuitize essential expenses, invest surplus

Required Minimum Distributions (US RMDs):
  → Start at age 73 (post-SECURE 2.0)
  → QCDs (Qualified Charitable Distributions): Satisfy RMD tax-free (up to $105,000/yr)
```

### Social Security Optimization (US)
- Break-even for delaying to 70 vs. 62: ~12–13 years
- If healthy and expect to live to 80+: Delay to 70 (maximize lifetime benefit)
- File-and-suspend strategies largely eliminated post-2015
- Spousal benefits: Up to 50% of higher earner's FRA benefit
