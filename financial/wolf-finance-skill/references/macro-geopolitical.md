## Geopolitical Risk Framework

### G-RISK Scoring (Geopolitical Risk Index)
```
Score each factor 1–5:
1. Military Conflict Probability (active conflict = 5, stable = 1)
2. Sanctions Intensity (comprehensive = 5, none = 1)
3. Trade War / Tariff Risk (escalating = 5, status quo = 1)
4. Political Transition Risk (contested election/coup = 5, stable = 1)
5. Resource Nationalism (nationalization risk = 5, protected = 1)

Total G-RISK: 5–10 = low | 10–15 = moderate | 15–20 = elevated | 20–25 = extreme

Portfolio adjustment:
  G-RISK > 20: Reduce EM/affected sector by 50%
  G-RISK 15–20: Hedge via options/inverse ETFs, max 50% full position
  G-RISK < 10: No special adjustment required
```

## Sanctions Framework

### Sanction Types & Market Impact
```
Primary Sanctions:
  → Direct restrictions on named entities/individuals (OFAC SDN)
  → Asset freezes, transaction bans
  → Impact: Complete capital market cutoff

Secondary Sanctions:
  → Penalize third-country entities doing business with sanctioned country
  → Creates "sanction risk" even for indirect exposure
  → Impact: Contagion to third-party countries

Sectoral Sanctions:
  → Target specific sectors (energy, defense, financial)
  → Impact: Sector-specific, manageable with restructuring

Comprehensive Sanctions (Full Embargo):
  → Russia (2022), Iran, North Korea, Cuba, Syria
  → Impact: Catastrophic for sanctioned country assets
```

### Sanction Screening (Portfolio)
1. **OFAC SDN list** — Direct ownership check (free: ofac.treasury.gov)
2. **EU Consolidated List** — EU-sanctioned entities
3. **UN Sanctions List** — Multi-lateral
4. **FinCEN watchlist** — US financial crimes enforcement
5. **Chainalysis/TRM Labs** — Crypto wallet screening

## Emerging Market Crisis Playbook

### EM Crisis Indicators (Pre-Crisis Checklist)
```
External Vulnerability:
  ✓ Current account deficit > 5% GDP
  ✓ FX reserves < 3 months of imports
  ✓ Short-term external debt > FX reserves
  ✓ External debt denominated in USD (currency mismatch)

Domestic Vulnerabilities:
  ✓ Budget deficit > 5% GDP with high debt-to-GDP
  ✓ Inflation > 10% (loss of monetary credibility)
  ✓ Banking system NPL ratio > 10%
  ✓ Political instability / election year populism
  ✓ Real estate bubble (price/income ratio extreme)

Market Stress Signals:
  ✓ CDS spreads >500bps (sovereign distress)
  ✓ Currency depreciation >20% in 3 months
  ✓ FX reserves falling >30% in 6 months
  ✓ Capital controls threats or implementation
  ✓ IMF program discussions (usually precede crisis)
```

### EM Crisis Playbook by Phase
| Phase | Signal | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| **Pre-Crisis** | 3+ vulnerability indicators | Reduce EM by 50%, hedge USD/EM pair |
| **Crisis Onset** | CDS spike, FX break | Exit or go short, buy CDS protection |
| **Acute Phase** | IMF bailout, capital controls | Stay out; track restructuring terms |
| **Recovery** | IMF program + reform credibility | Re-enter at steep discount (Brady bonds, post-crisis) |

```
China:
  → Property sector debt ($300T+ shadow banking)
  → Taiwan risk (military: tail risk, not base case)
  → USD/CNY floor at ~7.30 (PBoC defense)
  → Stimulus dependency: RRR cuts, LPR cuts, PSL bonds

India:
  → Structural growth story (demographics, tech services)
  → Currency: INR gradual depreciation trend
  → Premium valuation (Nifty P/E ~22× vs. EM average ~13×)
  → Regulatory risk: Capital gains taxes, SEBI rules

Indonesia (ID):
  → Commodity-linked (palm oil, coal, nickel)
  → OJK regulation: Crypto regulated under BAPPEBTI/OJK
  → BI (Bank Indonesia) rate sensitivity to USD strength
  → Reform risk: Pre/post election policy uncertainty

Brazil:

  → BRL highly sensitive to commodity prices + risk-off
  → High real rates = asset class in own right (Tesouro Direto)
  → Petrobras (PETR): Political interference risk
```

## Trade War Impact Analysis

### Tariff Transmission Mechanism
```
Tariff increase →
  1. Import prices rise (importer pays in first instance)
  2. Domestic producer prices rise (reduced competition)
  3. Consumer prices rise (CPI/PCE impact: ~0.1–0.3% per 10pp tariff)
  4. Retaliatory tariffs → export sector hurt
  5. Supply chain restructuring (6–24 month lag)

Winners: Domestic import-competing industries (steel, semiconductors)
Losers: Export-dependent industries, global supply chain companies

Portfolio Rotation on Trade War Escalation:
  AWAY FROM: Multinational consumer companies (Nike, Apple), EM supply chain
  TOWARD: Domestic industrial, onshoring beneficiaries, defense, energy
```

### China-US Decoupling Trade
```
Secular theme: Technology supply chain bifurcation
  → Semiconductors: TSMC, ASML restricted; Intel/Micron US fab investment
  → Rare earths: China controls 80%+ supply (strategic vulnerability)
  → Solar panels: China controls 90%+ of supply chain
  
Winners: US chip equipment (KLAC, LRCX), defense, domestic fab (Intel, TI)
Losers: US companies with China revenue >30% (NVDA, AAPL, QCOM short-term)
         Chinese tech (BABA, TCEHY) under regulatory + decoupling pressure

Monitoring: CHIPS Act spending, TSMC Arizona progress, Huawei restrictions
```

## Central Bank Geopolitical Response Playbook

```
Geopolitical shock → Central Bank Response Matrix:

Inflationary shock (oil price spike, supply chain):
  → Dilemma: Growth slows but inflation rises
  → Fed: Slower rate cuts (stagflation fear)
  → ECB: Accelerate cuts (growth-focused)
  → Safe haven: USD, CHF, JPY (flight to safety)

Deflationary shock (global demand collapse):
  → Fed: Emergency cuts, QE
  → All central banks: Coordinated easing
  → Safe haven: Long UST, gold

Financial stability shock (banking crisis):
  → Emergency facilities: Discount window, swap lines
  → Fed: Backstop liquidity (2023 SVB response)
  → ECB: OMT/TPI activation threat
  → Market impact: Initial shock → recovery as backstop kicks in
```
