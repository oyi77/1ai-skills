## Private Equity Deep Dive

### Deal Sourcing & Evaluation
```
Proprietary vs. Auction:
  Proprietary (sole-source): Higher chance of fair price, relationship-driven
  Auction (banker-run): Competitive, price premium, but process discipline

Investment Criteria (Typical Buyout):
  → EBITDA: $25M–$500M+ (depending on fund size)
  → EBITDA margins: >15% (or clear improvement path)
  → Market position: #1 or #2 in defensible niche
  → Revenue visibility: 60%+ recurring or contracted
  → Management quality: CEO stays or credible replacement
  → Exit optionality: Strategic buyers + PE secondaries + IPO path
```

### Value Creation Playbook (100-Day Plan)
1. **Financial engineering**: Optimize cap structure, refinance expensive debt
2. **Operational improvement**: EBITDA margin expansion (procurement, ops efficiency)
3. **Revenue acceleration**: Sales force build-out, geographic expansion, pricing
4. **M&A (Buy-and-Build)**: Platform + add-on acquisitions for multiple arbitrage
5. **Management upgrade**: Replace/supplement with operational expertise
6. **ESG positioning**: Increasingly required for institutional LP capital

### PE Fund Economics (2/20 Model)
```
Management Fee: 2% of committed capital (fund formation → investment period)
Carried Interest: 20% of profits above hurdle rate (typically 8% preferred return)
Waterfall: American (deal-by-deal) vs. European (whole-fund carry)

LPs receive:
  → Return of invested capital
  → Preferred return (8% annual)
  → 80% of remaining profits after GP catch-up
```

## Venture Capital Framework

### Stage Definitions & Typical Metrics
| Stage | Revenue | Valuation | Round Size | Key Risk |
|-------|---------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Pre-seed/Seed** | $0–$1M | $3–15M | $0.5–3M | Team, concept |
| **Series A** | $1–5M ARR | $15–60M | $5–20M | Product-market fit |
| **Series B** | $5–20M ARR | $50–200M | $20–50M | Scaling, unit economics |
| **Series C+** | $20M+ ARR | $200M–$1B+ | $50M+ | Market leadership |
| **Growth/Pre-IPO** | $50M+ ARR | >$500M | $100M+ | IPO readiness |

### VC Due Diligence Checklist
1. **Team**: Serial entrepreneurs? Domain expertise? Full-time commitment?
2. **Market**: TAM >$1B? Growing or declining? Timing of entry?
3. **Product**: Defensible moat? Customer love? Switching costs?
4. **Traction**: MoM growth rate, cohort retention, NPS
5. **Unit Economics**: LTV/CAC >3×, CAC payback <18mo (SaaS benchmark)
6. **Cap table**: Clean? Existing investors supportive? Option pool adequacy?
7. **Competitive moat**: Network effects, data, IP, regulation, brand?

### Power Law Returns (VC Math)
```
Top VC funds return 3–5× net over 10–12 years
BUT: ~60% of portfolio = write-offs
     ~20% = 1–2× (returning capital)
     ~15% = 2–5× (good outcomes)
     ~5% = >10× (fund returners)

Single 10× = most of fund returns
Single 100× (unicorn) = generational fund

Implication: Maximize upside optionality, not batting average
```

### Hedge Fund DD Questions
- What is the strategy's sharpe ratio over 5+ years?
- What is maximum drawdown? How long was recovery?

- Redemption terms: quarterly with 30-day notice? Gate provisions?

## Private Real Estate

### Asset Classes
```
Core:          Stabilized, low leverage (40–50%), 6–9% returns, pension-grade
Core Plus:     Slight value-add, 50–60% LTV, 9–13% returns
Value-Add:     Repositioning needed, 60–65% LTV, 13–18% returns
Opportunistic: Development/distress, 70%+ LTV, 18–25%+ returns
```

### Key Metrics
- **Cap Rate** = NOI / Property Value (inverse relationship with value)
- **NOI** = Revenue – Operating Expenses (before debt service)
- **LTV** = Loan / Value (higher = more risk)
- **DSCR** = NOI / Debt Service (>1.25× for healthy coverage)
- **IRR** includes leverage, time, distributions — compare to unlevered
- **Equity Multiple** = Total distributions / Invested equity

### Macro Impact on Real Estate
| Factor | Office/Retail | Residential | Industrial | Hotels |
|--------|--------------|-------------|------------|--------|
| Rising rates | Negative (cap rate expansion) | Negative | Moderate | Negative |
| Inflation | Mixed | Positive (rent growth) | Positive | Positive |
| WFH/remote | Very negative | Positive (suburban) | Neutral | Neutral |
| E-commerce | Retail negative | Neutral | Very positive | Neutral |

## Private Credit (Direct Lending)

### Market Overview
```
Direct Lending has replaced traditional bank lending for mid-market:
  → Borrower: $25M–$500M EBITDA companies (LBO financing, growth capital)
  → Lender: BDCs, private credit funds, CLOs
  → Typical yield: SOFR + 500–900bps (6–13% floating)
  → Senior secured first lien position (1st loss protection)

Key metrics:
  → Attachment point: Senior secured vs. unitranche vs. mezz
  → OID (original issue discount): Creates additional yield
  → PIK toggle: Allows borrower to defer cash interest (higher risk)
  → Financial covenants: Maintenance (tested quarterly) vs. incurrence
```

### Private Credit Risk Assessment
1. **Leverage**: Entry net debt/EBITDA (alarm at >6×)
2. **Coverage**: EBITDA/cash interest (alarm at <2×)
3. **Sponsor quality**: Will they inject equity if needed?
4. **Covenant protections**: Maintenance vs. incurrence (maintenance = lender protection)
5. **Collateral**: Asset coverage, IP, real estate
6. **Liquidity**: Revolver headroom, cash conversion cycle
